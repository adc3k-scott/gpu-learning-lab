# VIDEO 4: "800 Volts DC -- Why Every AI Factory After 2026 Will Use This"

**Duration:** ~2:30

### HOOK (0:00-0:05)

"Every GPU facility built before this year wastes 12 to 17 percent of its energy before it even reaches the chip. Ours wastes 4 percent. Here's why."

---

### SCRIPT

(0:05-0:35)

"Here's how power works in a traditional facility. Electricity comes in from the grid as AC -- alternating current. It hits a transformer. Loses 2%. Then a rectifier converts it to DC because every computer on Earth runs on DC. Loses another 3%. Then a UPS -- uninterruptible power supply -- converts it back to AC for distribution. Loses 3% more. Then at the rack, another rectifier converts it BACK to DC. Another 3%. You just lost 12 to 17 percent of your power doing absolutely nothing useful. Just converting back and forth."

[SHOW: Animated or annotated diagram of the traditional AC power path. Each step shows the conversion and the percentage lost. Use the power chain from /power-lsu. Highlight each loss in red.]

(0:35-1:05)

"Now here's what we're doing. Solar panels on the roof produce DC power. We run it through one DC-to-DC converter -- that's it -- straight to an 800 volt DC bus that feeds the racks directly. Two conversions total. 96 to 97 percent efficient. The natural gas generators produce AC, but they only feed the DC bus through one rectifier. The whole facility runs on 800 volts DC from end to end."

[SHOW: Our DC power path diagram. Solar panels -> DC-DC converter -> 800V bus -> rack. Show it clean, green, two steps. Then show the gas genset path feeding the same bus.]

(1:05-1:30)

"Think of it like plumbing. The old way is like running water through four different pipe sizes with adapters at every joint. Water pressure drops every time you change pipe size. Our way is one big pipe from the source to the rack. Same water, way more pressure. That 5% difference on a 2 megawatt solar array? That's 100 kilowatts saved continuously. Every hour the sun is shining, we're saving power that the old design just throws away as heat."

[SHOW: Simple plumbing analogy graphic -- garden hose with adapters (old) vs single fire hose (new). Then show the math: 100 kW saved x 1,800 sun hours = 180 MWh/year.]
