Why This Stack Makes Sense for AI Compute
Off-Grid by Design

Nat gas gensets run everything 24/7. LUS grid is backup only — never touched during normal operation. No grid rate exposure, no demand charges, no utility reliability risk. Henry Hub pipeline = uninterruptible fuel at the lowest cost in the country.

Cost Advantage

Prime nat gas generation at Henry Hub spot rates ($2–3/MMBtu) beats LUS grid rates every hour of every day. Bloom fuel cell + solar further reduce how hard the gensets work — lower fuel burn, longer engine life. Solar is zero marginal cost during daylight.

The Moat

No other Louisiana AI facility is built this way. "We run on our own power plant, the grid is our backup, and our fuel comes from the cheapest gas hub in North America" is a story no competitor in this region can tell.

Pipeline Site Advantage — Why Rural Louisiana Is Actually Better
Direct Nat Gas Tap

Pipeline sites have gas at the property. No truck delivery, no storage tanks. Generator fuel is continuous, uninterruptible, and at Henry Hub spot rates — cheapest in the country.

Land + Solar

Rural industrial sites have acreage. A 200 kW solar array needs ~1.5 acres. Sites like Kinder (27 acres) and Coteau (cane field) can run 1–5 MW of solar — more than enough to run the pod off solar during daylight hours.

No Grid Dependency

Nat gas (primary) + diesel (hurricane backup) + solar/battery (offset) = fully islanded microgrid. Grid is Layer 4 last resort — never primary. No utility rate exposure, no demand charges, no grid reliability risk.

Battery Storage — How It Works With Solar

The battery does three jobs simultaneously:

1
Stores Solar Daytime Production

Solar generates during daylight. Battery absorbs excess that the live load doesn't need. Releases it after sunset. Extends the "free power" window by 2–4 hours past sundown.

2
Peak Shaving — Reduces Demand Charges

LUS bills commercial customers on peak demand (highest 15-min draw in the month). Battery discharges during compute spikes to flatten the peak. Demand charges can be 30–50% of a commercial electric bill — peak shaving cuts this directly.

3
Instant Bridge During ATS Switchover

When grid fails, there is a 10–15 second gap while the genset starts and stabilizes. The battery carries 100% load during this window. Zero interruption to GPU jobs. Better than a UPS — more capacity, longer duration.

Automatic Transfer Switch
How Generator Switching Works — Step by Step
What actually happens when the grid goes down and the generators take over
Normal Operation (Grid Power)
1
Grid Powers Everything

LUS grid feeds the main switchboard at 480V 3-phase. ATS sits in "Normal" position. Generators are idle — not running, just waiting. Bloom fuel cell is running continuously (always on).

2
Battery Is Charged, Solar Is Producing

During daylight, solar feeds the load and charges the battery bank. Battery and Bloom reduce net grid draw. Everything is running in harmony.

3
Monitoring is Continuous

The ATS monitors grid voltage and frequency 100+ times per second. As long as grid is within spec, it does nothing. The second it detects a problem — it starts the clock.

Grid Failure Event Sequence
T+0
Grid Fails — ATS Detects in Milliseconds

Voltage drops below threshold. ATS confirms it's not a momentary sag (usually waits 0.5–2 seconds to confirm). Simultaneously sends start signal to both generators.

T+0 to T+10s
Battery Carries Full Load

Battery bank instantly picks up 100% of load. No interruption to GPU jobs — this is transparent to the compute. Bloom fuel cell continues running. This 10-second window is why you have batteries.

T+10–15s
Generators Reach Stable Speed and Voltage

Nat gas engines spin up. Voltage and frequency stabilize at 480V/60Hz. ATS verifies both generators are ready. Primary genset takes load. N+1 stays running in hot standby.

T+15s
ATS Transfers — Generators Now Powering Site

ATS throws the switch from grid to generator. This switch takes about 1/4 second. Battery resumes charging from genset output. Site is now running on nat gas — indefinitely, as long as Henry Hub has gas (it does).

Grid Returns
ATS Transfers Back to Grid Automatically

ATS detects stable grid return, waits a "cool down" period (usually 5–10 minutes to confirm stability), then transfers load back to grid. Generators cool down for 5 minutes then shut off. Total event is transparent to GPU workloads.

Key Questions to Ask Every Generator Vendor

1. What is the rated standby vs. prime power output?
Standby = backup only (hours). Prime = can run 24/7. For AI sites, you want prime-rated units. A 1,500 kW standby-rated unit may only do 1,350 kW at prime rating.

2. What is the THD (Total Harmonic Distortion) at full load?
GPU servers are sensitive. Want THD <5%. Ask for spec sheet.

3. What is the governor response time?
How fast does the engine react to sudden load changes? Important for AI workloads that ramp GPU utilization quickly.

4. Is ATS included or separate?
Some vendors bundle ATS with the genset package. Others sell separately. Confirm before pricing.

5. For used units — what are the hours and last service date?
Diesel-equivalent: under 15,000 hours is good. Get the last oil analysis report. This tells you everything about engine health.

6. Who services this unit in Louisiana?
For CAT — Ring Power (Baton Rouge, Lafayette). For Cummins — Cummins Mid-South. For Waukesha — INNIO Waukesha. For Generac — Depco Power. Know your service network before you buy.
