## What Is FUEL?

FUEL (Future Use of Energy in Louisiana) is an **NSF Regional Innovation Engine** — the largest and most competitive grant ever awarded by the U.S. National Science Foundation. It is NOT EPSCoR (it is a separate, newer NSF program called "NSF Engines"), but Louisiana IS EPSCoR-eligible, which strengthened the application.

- **Full name**: Louisiana Energy Transition Engine (FUEL)
- **NSF Award #**: 2315727
- **Program**: NSF Regional Innovation Engines (first-ever cohort, 10 teams nationwide)
- **Lead institution**: Louisiana State University
- **Announced**: January 29, 2024
- **Total potential**: Up to **$160M over 10 years** (NSF) + **$67.5M state match** (LED) = **$227.5M total**
- **Website**: [fuelouisiana.org](https://fuelouisiana.org)
- **NSF page**: [nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/regional-innovation-engines/portfolio/fuel-engine-louisiana](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/regional-innovation-engines/portfolio/fuel-engine-louisiana)

## The $45M Renewal (March 2026)

- Announced by **Governor Jeff Landry** at **CERAWeek Louisiana Future Energy** showcase
- **$45M funding renewal** spanning a **3-year operating period** (subject to congressional appropriations)
- Builds on **$15M in initial allocations** (first 2 years, $7.5M/year)
- Affirms NSF confidence in FUEL's advancement across core functions
- Announced alongside Louisiana's first **Nuclear Strategic Framework**

## What FUEL Funds (Core Research Areas)

1. **Carbon capture, transport, and storage (CCS)**
2. **Hydrogen** as alternative fuel
3. **CO2-to-products** — using carbon dioxide to produce low-carbon fuels and carbon-based products
4. **Water use and management** — sustainable water for energy
5. **Sustainable manufacturing** for clean energy
6. **Policy development** — economic and policy research for decarbonization pathways

### Three Core Functions
1. **Use-Inspired Research & Development** (Director: Ashwith Chilvery)
2. **Technology Commercialization** (Director: Stephen Loy)
3. **Workforce Development** (Director: Lacy McManus)
