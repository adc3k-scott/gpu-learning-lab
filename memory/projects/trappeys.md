# Trappeys Cannery — 200MW Solar - Natural Gas Compute Campus (THE CORE PROJECT)
Last updated: 2026-03-20

## What It Is
22-acre historic cannery campus on the Vermilion River in Lafayette, Louisiana. 46,000 sq ft of industrial warehouse space being transformed into Louisiana's first NVIDIA DSX-compliant AI compute facility. Solar-powered, water tower-cooled, American-made.

## Site Details
- Address: SE Evangeline Thruway corridor, Lafayette, LA
- GPS: 30.21356 N, 92.00163 W
- Acquisition cost: ~$1M for 22-acre campus
- 7 contributing structures (National Register eligible, period of significance 1930-1972)
- Half mile from UL Lafayette campus
- 30 miles from First Solar $1.1B panel factory (New Iberia)
- Industrial zoning — no rezoning needed
- Vermilion River frontage (connects to Atchafalaya Basin)

## History
- 1898: Bernard Francis Trappey starts bottling pepper sauce on Avery Island
- 1929: Purchases 8-acre Vermilion River site (former Baldwin Lumber / Star Salt)
- 1930: Canning operations begin — yams, okra, pepper sauce
- 1941-45: WWII — produced 12 MILLION pounds of candied yams for Allied forces
- 1968: Peak operations — 350-600 workers, 10M+ cans/year, global distribution
- 1992: Cannery closes after 62 years
- 2026: Reborn  200mw Solar - Natural compute campus

## 4-Layer Power Hierarchy (LOCKED)
1. Solar — Primary offset. 1,830 First Solar Series 7 panels (~1 MW)
2. Natural Gas — Backbone 24/7. Henry Hub pricing. ALWAYS running
3. Diesel Gensets — Emergency only. On-site fuel. Pipeline-independent
4. Grid (LUS) — SELL-BACK ONLY. NOT a power source. Excess goes back for revenue
