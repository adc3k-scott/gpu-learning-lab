# Skydio Gulf Coast Deployment Hub — KLFT Lafayette Regional Airport

**Source**: web/skydio-deck.html (24-slide pitch deck) + web/skydio-images/ (41 images) + Skydio_extracted/ (51 raw images/media)

## Proposal Summary
First Gulf Coast airport-based autonomous drone deployment hub at Lafayette Regional Airport (KLFT). Permanent Skydio regional hub for Louisiana and Gulf Coast. Airport-integrated Drone-as-First-Responder (DFR) + infrastructure response.

## Facility
Former PHI (Petroleum Helicopters) hangar — sits directly on KLFT south apron. Large clear-span structure, offices, fire suppression, industrial utilities already in place. No major renovation needed. GPS: 30.21256N, 91.99069W. Two sites: HS 1 (primary, former PHI hangar) and HS 2 (future dock farm expansion).

## Three Mission Pillars
1. **Public Safety** — DFR, search & rescue, disaster assessment
2. **Infrastructure Protection** — pipeline inspection, bridge inspection, utilities monitoring
3. **Emergency Response** — mapping, situational awareness, staging

## Enterprise Revenue Streams
Asset Inspection, Site Security, Surveying & Mapping

## Operational Model
- Public/private partnership — contracted services — regional support service
- Coordination with police, fire, emergency management
- NDAA-compliant platforms + grant-friendly structure
- Workflow: Incident → Dispatch → Dock launches drone → Drone on scene → Live data to agency → Agency responds

## System Architecture
- Skydio drone docks (autonomous — charge, stage, relaunch without human intervention)
- Weather hardening — high-wind spec, worst-case coastal weather from day one
- Power continuity — UPS + generator-ready, primary + backup internet/LTE/5G
- BVLOS readiness — designed for FAA framework as it evolves
- NDAA / secure supply chain compliance baked in

## Why Lafayette / KLFT
- FAA-controlled Class C airport, 24/7 access
- Central Gulf Coast — I-10/I-49 corridor, rapid reach to Baton Rouge, New Orleans, Lake Charles
- Long history of offshore helicopter operations (PHI, sea survival training)
- Strong oil/pipeline/refinery infrastructure
- University presence (UL Lafayette), technical workforce
- Uncongested airspace, repeatable flight corridors
- 450-mile Gulf Coast radius, <90 sec Skydio response time

## 4-Phase Roadmap
1. Facility + Partnerships — hangar prep, airport agreement, agency intros, equipment staging
2. Deployment Capability — dock install, command center, first responder integration, initial flights
3. Autonomous Network — BVLOS ops, full dock network, multi-agency dispatch, FAA authorization
4. Regional Expansion — additional Gulf Coast sites, statewide network, national reference model
