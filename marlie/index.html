02 — Government Funding Stack — Federal + State + Local
Federal
One Big Beautiful Bill Act

Signed July 4, 2025, the OBBBA creates the most favorable federal policy environment for domestic AI infrastructure in US history. It codifies FEOC (Foreign Entity of Concern) restrictions — any project with Chinese, Russian, Iranian, or North Korean supply chain involvement is disqualified from federal tax credits, grants, and incentives. Marlie I is purpose-built for full compliance: NVIDIA hardware manufactured in Texas (Foxconn Houston, Wistron Fort Worth), 100% US ownership, Louisiana-licensed GC. The OBBBA also added $500M to the BEAD broadband program and raised Gulf OCS revenue sharing to $650M/year — both of which directly benefit Louisiana AI infrastructure. The $500B Stargate public-private AI program runs parallel to the OBBBA; Marlie I's FEOC-clean structure qualifies it for Stargate-aligned federal procurement and financing channels.

Federal
BEAD Broadband Program

The Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) Program distributes federal funding to expand broadband infrastructure in underserved areas. Lafayette sits within a region with significant BEAD allocation. AI factories require massive bandwidth — and the presence of LUS Fiber, a city-owned gigabit network, creates a unique opportunity to layer BEAD-funded backhaul expansion directly into the Marlie I connectivity plan. LUS Fiber runs 0.8 miles from the site. Direct fiber connection to the AI factory accelerates BEAD project eligibility for the City of Lafayette while simultaneously solving Marlie I's connectivity requirements.

Federal
Gulf OCS Revenue

The OBBBA raised Louisiana's annual Gulf of America OCS revenue-sharing cap from $500M to $650M/year through 2034 — a 30% increase in the state's offshore energy dividend. Louisiana's share (~$200M/year at cap) funds the Coastal Master Plan, the infrastructure backbone that protects sites like Marlie I from long-term flood risk. Simultaneously, Louisiana's offshore energy production — the same natural gas supply chain feeding Marlie I's generators — is the economic engine behind this revenue. Henry Hub, the world's most liquid natural gas trading point, is 40 miles from Marlie I in Erath, LA. Marlie I's fuel costs are benchmarked to the most competitive gas price on earth.

State
Louisiana Act 730

Louisiana Act 730 (effective July 1, 2024) provides a 20-year state and local sales and use tax rebate on qualifying AI factory equipment, software, construction materials, and installation — with a 10-year renewal option (30 years total). Qualification requires $200M+ in capital investment and 50+ new permanent jobs certified by Louisiana Economic Development. Marlie I's capital plan is structured to meet both thresholds. Once certified, a Direct Payment Number eliminates tax liability on the single largest cost in the project: GPU hardware. Stackable with the Quality Jobs Program (6% payroll rebate, 10 years) and LED FastStart (free customized workforce training). Combined with Louisiana's #1-ranked industrial electricity rates, Act 730 makes Marlie I's per-token economics unbeatable.

We engineer for what can happen, not just what usually happens. MARLIE I is designed to keep running through a Category 4 landfall — five independent power layers, stored fuel for 96+ hours on-site, and zero dependence on any single utility or pipeline.

1
LUS POWER — UTILITY GRID TIE
Lafayette Utilities System municipal grid. Primary power during commissioning, servicing windows, and low-draw periods. Cooperative interconnect agreement — municipal utility. MARLIE I draws minimally from the grid at operating capacity, but the tie-in is the legal and operational safety net required by lenders and by code.

BLOOM ENERGY — ON-SITE FUEL CELLS

Runs 24/7/365 without interruption. Offsets 10.8% of Phase 1 facility load on an ongoing basis. Made in Newark, Delaware — 100% domestic.

CAT G3520H — NATURAL GAS PRIME POWER (PARALLEL N+1)
2× Caterpillar G3520H — 2.5 MW each, 5 MW total installed capacity. Both units operate in parallel at moderate load, providing true N+1 redundancy across the full build — if either unit requires service, the other carries the facility without interruption. EPA prime/continuous certified — not standby-restricted. 10-second full block load from cold start. Natural gas / up to 25% hydrogen blend. Manufactured: Caterpillar Large Engine Center, Lafayette, Indiana. 5,500 parts from 500+ U.S. suppliers across 33 states. OBBBA domestic content certified.

UPS — BATTERY RIDE-THROUGH

Zero perceived downtime to hosted workloads during any power source switch.

DIESEL EMERGENCY BACKUP — HURRICANE LAYER
Last-resort layer — independent of natural gas pipelines, utility grid, and all external infrastructure. Activates when gas supply is interrupted by hurricane, pipeline shutdown, or force majeure.

Phase 1: 1× Cat C175-16 diesel emergency standby — 3,365 kW (3.4 MW). Covers full Phase 1 facility load as a single unit. On-site 30,000-gallon double-wall UL 142 diesel storage tank. Fuel consumption: ~229 gal/hr at full load. Runtime: 110+ hours — NFPA 110 Level 1 (96-hour minimum) exceeded.
Full Build: 2× Cat C175-16 diesel (N+1) — 6,730 kW operational with any one unit offline. Covers 6.34 MW full-build requirement. Two 30,000-gallon UL 142 tanks (60,000 gal total). 96-hour runtime at full two-floor load per NFPA 110 Level 1.

Manufactured: Caterpillar Large Engine Center — Lafayette, Indiana. C175 family is exclusive to the Lafayette LEC. 10,000th C175 engine rolled off the line December 2023.
