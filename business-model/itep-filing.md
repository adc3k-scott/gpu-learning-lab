# Louisiana ITEP Filing — ADC Willow Glen Terminal

## What Is ITEP?
**80% property tax abatement** on new capital investment (buildings + equipment, NOT land) for 5 years, renewable for another 5 years at 80%. Mega-projects ($150M+) may qualify for 93-100%.

## Dollar Impact

### Example: $150M Qualifying Investment
```
Assessed Value = $150M × 15% = $22.5M
Without ITEP: $22.5M × 90 mills = $2.025M/year in property tax
With ITEP (80%): 20% × $2.025M = $0.405M/year
Annual Savings = $1.62M/year

5-Year Total:  $8.1M saved
10-Year Total: $16.2M saved (with renewal)
```

At 93% mega-project rate: **$9.4M over 5 years, $18.8M over 10 years.**

## CRITICAL: NAICS Code Problem

**The blocker:** ITEP requires NAICS codes beginning with 31, 32, or 33 (Manufacturing). ADC's natural classification is NAICS 518200 (Data Center / Computing Infrastructure Services) — which does NOT qualify.

### Three Pathways

**Pathway A: "Emerging Industry" Classification (RECOMMENDED)**
- Louisiana rules allow LED to classify emerging industries as manufacturing if:
  1. Novel/emerging technology sector
  2. Manufacturing-like activities (assembly, configuration, optimization)
  3. Value-added transformation (raw GPU hardware → optimized inference pipeline → tokens)
  4. Products placed into commerce (inference tokens sold to customers)
- LED sends to Louisiana Workforce Commission (LWC) for NAICS verification
- **Requires pre-filing consultation with LED to validate approach**

**Pathway B: Hardware Manufacturing (NAICS 3344 or 333)**
- If ADC builds custom GPU enclosures, power distribution, or cooling hardware on-site
- Requires demonstrable hardware manufacturing process
- Harder argument with pre-built NVIDIA racks
- **ADC 3K pod manufacturing could strengthen this angle**

**Pathway C: Joint Venture with Hardware Partner**
- Partner with a true manufacturer (cooling, enclosures) that carries NAICS 3344/333
- That partner receives ITEP; ADC benefits via CEA terms
- Less direct but proven in other states

## Filing Process — Step by Step

### Pre-Filing (Weeks 1-4)
1. Contact LED (Kristin Johnson) with NAICS question
2. Gather required documents (see checklist below)
3. Get legal property description from WGT
4. Draft manufacturing justification letter (2-3 pages)

### File Application (Week 4-6)
- **Portal:** FastLane NextGen (FLNG) — https://fastlaneng.louisianaeconomicdevelopment.com/
- All online, no paper forms
- **MUST FILE BEFORE BREAKING GROUND** — no exceptions, no grandfathering

### LED Review (Weeks 6-10)
- LED reviews for completeness + eligibility (2-4 weeks)
- May request additional info (90-day response window)
- Routes to LDR for tax impact review
- Routes to LWC if NAICS is non-standard

### Local ITEP Committee (Weeks 10-14)
- LED forwards to Iberville Parish Local ITEP Committee
- Committee posts public notice (15 days)
- Public meeting within 30 days of notice
- Committee votes on exemption percentage recommendation
- **Non-binding** — BCI and Governor have final say
- Committee members: Parish Council rep, School Board rep, Sheriff (Brett Stassi), Assessor (ex-officio)

### BCI + Governor Approval (Weeks 14-22)
- Board of Commerce & Industry reviews all materials
- BCI votes to approve, deny, or modify
- Governor issues final approval
- ITEP Contract issued

### Total Timeline
| Scenario | Duration |
|----------|----------|
| Best case (clean filing) | 10-19 weeks (2.5-4.5 months) |
| Realistic (NAICS pre-qualification) | 14-23 weeks (3.5-5.5 months) |
| Worst case (multiple RFI rounds) | 6-9 months |

## Other Louisiana Incentives (Stack with ITEP)

### Act 730 — Data Center Sales Tax Exemption (effective July 1, 2024)
- State AND local sales/use tax exemption on equipment and software
- **Term:** 20 years + 10-year renewal option
- **Requirements:** $200M+ capital investment + 50 permanent jobs by July 1, 2029
- **Note:** "Data center" framing is OK for Act 730 (different from ITEP). May want separate entity structure.

### Quality Jobs Program
- 5-6% payroll rebate for 10 years on new qualifying jobs

### LED FastStart
- Free customized workforce recruitment, training, and development

### Federal ITC (Solar + Battery)
- 30% base + 10% domestic content + 10% energy community = up to 50% CAPEX reduction
- Iberville Parish (former fossil fuel site) likely qualifies for energy community bonus

## Critical Rules

1. **File BEFORE breaking ground** — any physical work on-site (site prep, equipment delivery/installation) disqualifies you
2. **Land is NOT eligible** — only buildings and equipment
3. **"Data center" framing will get rejected for ITEP** — always say "AI factory" / "token manufacturing facility"
4. **Pre-qualify NAICS with LED** before filing to avoid rejection
5. **Don't order major equipment** until application is in LED's queue
