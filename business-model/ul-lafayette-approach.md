# UL Lafayette Partnership — Approach Strategy

## Why This Matters
UL Lafayette is the single biggest domino for ADC. It unlocks:
- **Anchor tenant** — de-risks financing, proves demand
- **Workforce pipeline** — 40K+ students, 2 miles from Trappeys
- **Academic credibility** — opens doors to NVIDIA, government, grants
- **Federal funding** — joint NSF/DOE proposals neither party reaches alone
- **ITEP support** — university partnership strengthens local committee case

## What UL Lafayette Has (Strong Foundation)

### Programs
- **PhD in Applied Computing & Information Sciences** — first in Louisiana, launched 2026
- **MS in Informatics** — 33 credit hours, data science/ML/cybersecurity
- **Data Science & AI Bootcamp** — 24 weeks, workforce re-skilling
- **AI/ML Undergraduate Emphasis** — pre-professional pipeline

### Research Centers (Hungry for GPU)
- **CAAI** (Center for Applied AI) — Dr. Gottumukkala, NSF AHeAD Center, health/disaster/supply chain
- **IRI** (Informatics Research Institute) — Dr. Henry Chu, 5 constituent centers, $225M+ annual R&D
- **CACS** (Center for Advanced Computer Studies) — grad research hub

### NSF Grants (Real Money)
- **NSF FUEL Consortium** — up to $160M over 10 years (UL co-lead with LSU, Tulane, Xavier). Energy transition, hydrogen, carbon capture. Dr. Jonathan Raush is PI.
- **NSF AHeAD Center** — multi-university (UF, Tulane, Georgia Tech). AI for healthcare decisions.
- **AI for Geosciences** — phytoplankton monitoring, computational marine science

### Current Computing (THE GAP)
- **Firebird HPC Cluster** — CPU only (Intel Xeon, no GPU), shared with Swarthmore College
- **LONI Oliver** — Dell Linux cluster, part of Louisiana Optical Network
- **Cloud compute** — buying from AWS/Google/Azure for research
- **No NVIDIA GPU infrastructure** — this is ADC's value add

## How to Frame the Partnership

### DO NOT say:
- "Buy GPUs from us" (transactional)
- "Colo facility" (wrong framing)
- "Data center" (never)

### DO say:
**"ADC Compute Partnership: AI Research Acceleration for Louisiana"**

### What UL Lafayette Gets
1. Preferential access to local NVIDIA GPUs (vs. buying AWS at 3-5x cost)
2. Graduate research pipeline on real HPC infrastructure (FUEL, AHeAD, geosciences)
3. Degree program enhancement (PhD in Applied Computing + Informatics MS get real labs)
4. Joint NSF proposals with infrastructure partner (more $ than university alone)
5. Carnegie R1 momentum (GPU infrastructure strengthens R&D profile)
6. Faculty recruitment advantage (computational researchers want local GPU access)

### What ADC Gets
1. Anchor tenant — long-term commitment de-risks financing
2. Academic credibility — opens NVIDIA, government, SBIR/STTR doors
3. Workforce pipeline — hire graduates directly, train employees
4. Grant co-application — joint NSF/DOE/DARPA proposals
5. Proven model — "First NVIDIA-anchored compute hub in Louisiana"

## Approach Path (Step by Step)

### Phase 1: Warm Introduction (Weeks 1-2)

**Best path:** Through LEDA (Lafayette Economic Development Authority)
- URL: https://lafayette.org/
- Angle: "ADC wants to establish an AI compute facility at Trappeys with UL Lafayette as anchor tenant. We need UL stakeholders involved early."
- LEDA already partners with UL, knows both ecosystems

**Alternative:** Greater Lafayette Commerce (https://www.greaterlafayettecommerce.com/)

**If no warm intro available:** Go direct to Dr. Kolluru. He's brand new and meeting with business leaders.

### Phase 2: Technical Alignment (Weeks 2-4)

**Meeting 1: Leadership (Provost + Dean + Computing Director)**
- Attendees: Hebert, Khattab, Margala, Scott
- Agenda: ADC specs, UL research needs, degree integration, workforce pipeline
- Output: Letter of intent to explore partnership

**Meeting 2: Research Leaders**
- Attendees: Gottumukkala (CAAI), Chu (IRI), Raush (FUEL), Scott
- Agenda: AHeAD GPU needs, FUEL energy modeling, geosciences ML, joint NSF proposals
- Output: 2-3 research projects with GPU requirements identified

### Phase 3: Deal Structure (Weeks 4-8)

**Recommended: Hybrid Model**
- UL Lafayette as official "Research Partner"
- Some subsidized capacity for FUEL, AHeAD, etc.
- Revenue share on commercial inference jobs
- Joint grant proposals (NSF, DOE)
- Workforce training in exchange for compute access

**Target:** Letter of intent within 60-90 days. Pilot project (e.g., FUEL energy modeling on 10 GPUs) within 6 months.
