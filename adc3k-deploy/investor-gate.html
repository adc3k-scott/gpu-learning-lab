A Once-in-a-Generation Infrastructure Opportunity

The Big Beautiful Bill opened the floodgates. The federal government wants AI infrastructure built in America — and they want it in Louisiana. Why? Because no state in the country has cheaper energy. Natural gas at Henry Hub pricing. Solar panels manufactured in-state. And enough wasted gas flaring across the oilfield to power an entire AI economy.

Louisiana has been the backbone of America's energy infrastructure for over a century. Oil. Gas. Petrochemical. Power generation. AI compute is just the next form of energy infrastructure — and it's coming home to the state that knows how to build it.

Why right now? Because everything before two years ago is obsolete.

If it wasn't built on NVIDIA Blackwell or newer, it's already old equipment. The technology that makes AI factories work — 800V DC power, liquid-cooled GPU racks, inference optimization — didn't exist before 2024. Everything prior is like running an Atari when the world needs supercomputers.

We are now in the agentic era. AI systems don't just answer questions anymore — they think, act, and work autonomously. Every time an AI agent processes a request, it generates inference tokens. Inference is the new commodity. And it requires massive compute infrastructure that Louisiana is uniquely positioned to provide.

The grants are allocated. The manufacturers are in-state. The technology is brand new. The sites are identified. This is a lifetime investment opportunity — brand new infrastructure with the latest equipment that our schools need, our cities need, and our state needs to stay competitive.

Why Louisiana? Why not Texas or Virginia?

Cheapest natural gas in North America — Henry Hub is 60 miles away. Massive amounts of gas wasted in oilfield flaring that can be captured and used. First Solar just built a $1.1 billion panel factory in New Iberia. Eaton manufactures 800V DC power systems in Nacogdoches, TX — right next door. NVIDIA's chip fabrication is in Texas. Elon Musk just announced Terafab — another chip facility in Texas.

The entire supply chain is within driving distance. Every piece of equipment is American-made. Nothing is outsourced. We use every Louisiana company, every Louisiana worker, every Louisiana resource we can get our hands on.

This is our backyard. We know where to build. We know the processes. We know the people. We're not coming from out of state — we live here, we build here, and we're not waiting for permission.
