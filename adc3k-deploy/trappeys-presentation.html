Heritage
4,000 Years.
One Piece of Land.
~2000 BCAtakapa-Ishak people — the Ciwat (Alligator Band) — live along the Vermilion River for thousands of years
1898B.F. Trappey Sr. founds Trappey & Sons on the banks of the Vermilion — hot sauce, peppers, canning operations
1920sTexaco leases to Baldwin Lumber & Star Salt. Industrial site established.
1927Great Flood of 1927 — 10 feet of water. Buildings damaged but survive. Built to last.
1930Trappey's Cannery opens full scale. Builds its own electric powerhouse. 350-600 workers at peak. One of Acadiana's largest employers.
1941-45World War II — 12 million pounds of candied yams shipped to US troops overseas. One of the largest food production contracts in Louisiana history. Peak production: 10 million+ cans per year.
1950-80sDecades of continuous food processing. National distribution. Trappey's becomes a household name across the South.
1991Plant closes after 62 years of continuous operation. Sits empty for 30+ years.
2026ADC restores it as mission critical AI infrastructure for all of Lafayette. Same building. Same power independence. New mission.

How It Works
Liquid Cooled.
Not Air Conditioned.
NVIDIA NVL72 Racks
72 GPUs + 36 CPUs per rack. Factory-sealed liquid cooling loops at 45°C. No server fans. No hot aisles. Installed in 2 hours — plug in, turn on.
Water Tower
15,000 gallons of thermal buffer. Absorbs heat spikes. The hot water from the racks cycles through the tower and radiates heat naturally. No massive chillers.
Vermilion River
Natural free-cooling resource running alongside the site. Combined with liquid cooling, achieves PUE 1.03 — meaning 97% of every watt goes directly to computing.
100% Green Design
First Solar on every rooftop. Natural gas backbone (cleanest fossil fuel). No coal. No nuclear. Excess power sold back to LUS. Net positive for Lafayette's grid.
