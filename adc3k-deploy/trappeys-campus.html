Walk the Campus

The Trappeys Cannery campus on the Vermilion River in Lafayette. 46,000 square feet of industrial warehouse space. National Register eligible. Built for heavy manufacturing in 1930 — being reborn as Louisiana's first AI factory.
Overview

The Full Campus

22 acres on the Vermilion River between SE Evangeline Thruway and Guidry Street. Seven contributing structures from the original cannery operations (1930-1992). Massive concrete pad for infrastructure yard. Existing power feed. Industrial zoning.

Landmark

The Water Tower

The iconic Trappeys water tower — visible from Evangeline Thruway. National Register contributing structure. In the AI factory, this becomes part of the cooling infrastructure: thermal mass for heat rejection from NVIDIA liquid-cooled racks.

Compute Space

46,000 Sq Ft of Open Floor

The metal warehouse interiors are exactly what you want for an AI factory: massive open floor plates with steel column structure, high ceilings for airflow, skylights for natural light during construction, and concrete slab floors rated for industrial loads. No walls to knock down. No HVAC to rip out. Drop in racks and run power.
ComputeMain Warehouse InteriorSteel column grid on concrete slab. High ceiling with skylights. This floor gets NVIDIA liquid-cooled NVL72 racks in rows between columns. 36-84 racks depending on power phase.
ComputeWarehouse North EndAnother angle showing the depth of the space. The column grid provides natural rack row alignment. Industrial floor handles the weight of fully-loaded NVL72 racks (2,800+ lbs each).
Partner Offices + Community

The Brick Buildings

The original brick cannery structures. Loading docks, processing rooms, offices. These become partner offices (Siemens, Eaton, First Solar), community space, riverwalk access, and the campus welcome center. 45% combined historic tax credits apply to qualified rehabilitation.

Power

Existing Infrastructure

The site was built for heavy industrial power. Existing 3-phase electrical service, utility poles with transformers, natural gas stubs. The infrastructure yard (concrete pad) receives gas generators, switchgear, battery storage, and solar inverters without new foundations.
PowerExisting Electrical FeedUtility poles with transformers serving the campus. Three-phase power confirmed. No transmission buildout required — upgrade the service, don't build it.
PowerUtility CorridorPower lines and utility infrastructure along the campus edge. Gas generators (Caterpillar) and switchgear (Powell Industries) deploy on the concrete pad adjacent to this feed.
PowerPower Feed DetailClose-up of the existing electrical infrastructure. 4-layer power hierarchy: Solar (offset) > Natural Gas (backbone) > Diesel (emergency) > Grid (sell-back only).
Riverwalk

Vermilion River Frontage

The campus sits directly on the Vermilion River — connected to the Atchafalaya Basin, North America's largest floodplain swamp. The riverwalk is not just aesthetic: it's a community amenity, a cooling water source, and the ecological story that connects this AI factory to Lafayette's Cajun heritage.

History

Industrial Heritage

Remnants of 62 years of canning operations. The Trappeys cannery produced over 12 million pounds of candied yams for WWII Allied forces, employed up to 600 workers, and distributed 10 million+ cans per year worldwide. Nine contributing structures, all National Register eligible.
HeritageOriginal EquipmentIndustrial pump from the canning era. Equipment like this tells the story of a century of Louisiana manufacturing — from yams to AI inference tokens.
