Site History

300 Years of Powering Louisiana

This place has been powering Louisiana since before Louisiana was a state. We're just giving it a new mission.

Pre-1699
Indigenous Era

Bayogoula tribes inhabit the Mississippi River corridor. The land that will become Iberville Parish serves as a vital waterway and trading route for centuries.

1699
French Exploration

Pierre Le Moyne d'Iberville explores the Mississippi. The parish is named after him — the first European claim on this stretch of river.

1800s
"Sweet Iberville"

Iberville Parish becomes Louisiana's leading sugarcane producer. Willow Glen Plantation is established — the name that endures to this day.

1960
2,200 MW Power Station

Entergy builds one of the largest power stations in the South. Five generating units. For 56 years, this site powers Louisiana's industrial corridor.

2016
Decommissioned

Entergy decommissions the Willow Glen generating station. The turbines go silent after more than half a century of continuous operation.

2020
Demolition & Remediation

Zydeco acquires 700 acres. Genover demolition removes 30,000+ tons of steel and 1,000,000+ lbs of copper. The site is cleared and ready for its next chapter.

2026
Tiger Compute Campus

ADC proposes Tiger Compute Campus. Same land. Same grid connection. Same Mississippi River. New mission — AI compute for Louisiana's next 300 years.

Energy Innovation — Not Just Efficient, Regenerative

Traditional facilities waste 60% of fuel energy as heat. ADC recovers it. Absorption chillers convert waste heat INTO cooling — eliminating the electric chillers that consume 30-40% of facility power in Louisiana's climate. Organic Rankine Cycle turbines convert exhaust heat into 8-11 MW of additional electricity. Mississippi River provides unlimited cooling capacity. Renewable diesel from Diamond Green Diesel (60 river miles away) provides 65-90% carbon reduction with zero generator modifications. Hydrokinetic river turbines generate supplemental power from the Mississippi's current. Every BTU does double duty. Every layer recovers what the last one spent.
