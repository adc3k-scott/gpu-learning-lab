The Vermilion River is a site amenity, not a thermal loop

Gulf Coast surface water peaks at 30–33°C in summer and the Vermilion is tidally influenced at Lafayette — so the river does not serve the cooling system. It does serve the restoration: rebuilt seawall, public riverwalk, and the defining photograph of the campus.

Architecture

11 Replicated Blocks. One Cassette Product. Permanent Island.

The campus is 11 electrically independent blocks. Each block runs 4 gensets, one step-down transformer, one 480 VAC switchboard, and 4 cassette feeds. Blast radius is contained to one block. BESS and solar tie to the same 480 VAC bus. 800 VDC exists only inside each cassette, where it belongs — right next to the GPUs.

Energy Sovereignty

No Grid. No Interconnect Queue. No Transmission Buildout.

Behind-the-meter permanent island from day one. The facility does not depend on utility capacity to come online. LUS Pinhook Substation sits adjacent to the site and remains a future option at the utility's cost and schedule, not ours. The single external input is natural gas.

The block topology gives the facility N+1 redundancy at block level without relying on utility backup. The BESS is a stabilizer — always active, sub-cycle response via Hitachi AMPS PCS SiC inverter — not a standby system. Sub-100 ms GPU transients are handled inside each cassette by rack-integrated capacitance and BBU. Nothing waits on the grid.

Market Demand

GPU Supply Is the Constraint, Not Demand

The market signal is structural and persistent — available GPU inventory across major cloud marketplaces is consistently tight. We don't publish specific quoted capacity or pricing figures that move daily; we can walk through current snapshots in person.

Structural Market Condition

NVIDIA Allocation Is the Gating Input

Vera Rubin GPU allocation from NVIDIA is the gating variable for Stage 1 ramp — more than financing, more than permitting, more than construction. The site is designed to absorb allocation as it becomes available, cassette by cassette, rather than waiting for a single batched delivery.

This is why the replicated-block architecture matters: Block 1 can energize with 4 cassettes while Blocks 2–11 remain in staged construction. The facility starts generating tokens as GPUs arrive, not as the last generator is commissioned.
