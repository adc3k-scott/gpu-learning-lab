Site History

Trappeys Cannery — Since 1929

A cannery that fed soldiers in World War II becomes the AI factory that powers Louisiana's future.

1920s
Origins

Texas Company leased the 8-acre tract to Baldwin Lumber and Star Salt. Industrial use from the beginning — heavy structures, rail access, river frontage.

1929
B.F. Trappey's Sons Purchased the Tract

The Trappey family acquired the property and began building the cannery complex that still stands today. Four industrial buildings, water tower, infrastructure yard.

1930–1981
Cannery Operations · WWII Production

Operated as a full-scale cannery for over 50 years. During World War II, the facility produced 12 million pounds of candied yams for Allied forces. Peak employment in the hundreds.

1950
Water Tower Constructed

The iconic water tower was built for industrial cooling and fire suppression. 75 years later, it becomes the cooling backbone for an AI factory — same tower, new mission.

1991
Plant Closed

The cannery shut down after six decades of operation. The buildings sat empty for 30+ years. The structures endured — steel frame, concrete, industrial-grade construction.

2026
Proposed AI Factory

Same buildings. Same water tower. New mission. The initiative proposes converting the historic cannery into Louisiana's first solar-powered AI factory. National Register of Historic Places approved — 9 contributing buildings, 45–55% historic tax credits.
