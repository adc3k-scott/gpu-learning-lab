The Big Idea

Every component in an NVIDIA AI factory is a smart device — not dumb hardware waiting for instructions. Each chip, switch, and card is a little computer with its own management built in.

They all exist for one reason: remove overhead from the GPUs so the GPUs do nothing but compute. That's accelerated computing.

When every piece of silicon is designed to talk to every other piece natively — nothing is translating, nothing is waiting, nothing is wasted.

In Kyber NVL144: NVSwitch blades sit behind the midplane, doubling the domain. Without this chip, you have 72 separate GPUs. With it, you have one GPU with 72x the memory.

Kyber Rack Architecture 2027The next-gen rack that changes everything — vertical blades, passive midplane, 144-576 GPUs

Kyber Midplane BACKBONE
72-layer copper PCB. 87,000+ NVLink pins. Zero cables.
The core innovation. A passive 72-layer copper PCB that sits in the center of the rack. Compute blades plug in from the front, NVLink switch blades plug in from the rear. 87,000+ NVLink signal pins connect everything through the board — no cables, no connectors, no signal loss. Eliminates 2 miles of cabling per rack. Every connection is a direct copper trace at PCB-level signal integrity.
Compute Blades (Switchblades) FRONT
Vertical GPU blades. Hot-swap from the front.
Vera Rubin Ultra GPUs mounted on vertical blades that slide into the front of the rack and mate with the Kyber midplane. Each blade carries GPUs + HBM4 + Vera CPU. Orientation is vertical (not horizontal like traditional servers) — shorter signal paths, better cooling airflow, higher density. Hot-swappable: pull a blade, replace it, zero downtime for the rest of the rack.
NVLink Switch Blades REAR
NVSwitch 6 arrays. Plug into the back of the midplane.
Rear-mounted blades carrying NVSwitch 6 ASICs that connect to compute blades through the midplane. Creates a full-bisection NVLink fabric across all 144 GPUs. Every GPU can talk to every other GPU at full bandwidth simultaneously. The midplane is the highway; the switch blades are the interchanges.
NVL144 Rack (Single Kyber) 144 GPUs
2x the NVLink domain. One rack, one brain.
144 Vera Rubin Ultra GPUs in a single NVLink domain — 2x the NVL72. ~600 kW per rack. 800 VDC power delivery (not traditional AC). All liquid cooled. The midplane eliminates the cabling bottleneck that limited previous gens to 72 GPUs per domain.
NVL1152 Pod (8x Kyber) 1,152 GPUs
8 Kyber racks linked. The Vera Rubin POD.
1,152 Rubin Ultra GPUs across 8 Kyber racks. ~20,000 NVIDIA dies. 1.2 quadrillion transistors. 60 exaFLOPS. 10 PB/s scale-up bandwidth. The complete SuperPOD unit — 40 total racks including compute, networking, storage, and CPU racks. ~4.8 MW per pod. This is the compute brick ADC's DSX-compliant facilities are designed to receive. Layer 1: The MuscleWhere the work gets done

GPUs (Blackwell Now / Rubin H2 2026) COMPUTE
The workers. They eat data.
What they do: AI training, inference, simulation, rendering — all the heavy lifting. Blackwell: 20 PFLOPS FP4. Vera Rubin: 50 PFLOPS FP4. They don't waste a single cycle on networking, security, or storage — that's everyone else's job.
HBM Memory (HBM3e / HBM4) MEMORY
The fuel tank. Right next to the engine.
What it does: High Bandwidth Memory stacked directly on the GPU package. Rubin: 288 GB per GPU at 22 TB/s (3x Blackwell bandwidth). The model weights and training data live here so the GPU never waits for memory.
Groq LPU (Language Processing Unit) INFERENCE
The sprinter. Decode-only, zero-latency token output.
NEW at GTC 2026. NVIDIA acquired Groq for $20B. LP30 die, Samsung 4nm. 512 MB SRAM, 150 TB/s bandwidth, 1.2 PFLOPS FP8. No HBM — pure SRAM, deterministic linear dataflow. GPUs do prefill (reasoning), Groq does decode (token output). Dynamo splits the work. 35x tokens-per-watt. Like turbo + supercharger — different power bands, same output.

Layer 2: The Nervous SystemHow GPUs talk to each other

NVLink 6 + NVLink Switch GPU-TO-GPU
Direct GPU-to-GPU highway. No detours.
What it does: Connects GPUs within a rack at 3,600 GB/s per GPU (Vera Rubin, 2x Blackwell). NVL72: 9 NVSwitch 6 chips, 72 GPUs act as one. Kyber NVL144 (2027): NVSwitch blades behind the midplane, 144 GPUs in a single NVLink domain. 87,000+ copper traces, zero cables.
InfiniBand (Quantum Switches) NODE-TO-NODE
The backbone. Connects every rack in the building.
What it does: Quantum-X800: 144 ports x 800 Gb/s, sub-100ns latency. 14.4 TFLOPS in-network SHARP v4 compute — math operations right on the switch fabric. Data gets reduced before it hits the GPU. Managed by UFM. Still the king for large-scale training. Spectrum-X Ethernet now available for mid-size inference clusters.
BlueField-4 DPUs SECURITY + I/O
A full ARM computer on a network card. Now 4x stronger.
What it does: Offloads all networking, security, encryption, firewall, and storage I/O from the CPU/GPU. Runs DOCA. BF-4: 64 Arm cores, 128 GB memory, 800 Gb/s, PCIe Gen 6. The GPU never sees a packet header or a firewall rule.
