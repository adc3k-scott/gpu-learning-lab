Site Plan

S-001 — Site Overview

0.60 Acres  |  3 Parcels
0.60 acres, 3 parcels, owned outright. Building houses Floor 1 compute (10 NVL72 racks) and Floor 2 NOC/Command Hub. Three ADC 3K pods side by side on the adjacent concrete pad — each a 40-ft ISO container with 10 rack positions (8 compute + 1 network + 1 storage). Bloom SOFC cluster on remaining pad space — fuel cells are quiet with no combustion exhaust, no standoff distance required. Gas line: short run to main tap, same infrastructure corridor as Trappeys 0.5 mi east. Hub position: Trappeys east, KLFT northwest, downtown north.
Architectural

A-001 — Floor Plans

Floor 1: Compute  |  Floor 2: NOC / Command
Floor 1 runs 10 NVL72 racks in a single row — cold aisle at front, hot aisle at rear, CoolIT CDU pair at end wall. 720 Blackwell GPUs, 1,300 kW IT load. Floor 2 is the command hub: NOC video wall with full visibility into building racks and all 3 exterior pods, network/server closet with InfiniBand spine and fiber connections to Trappeys and KLFT, and a briefing room with view of the compute floor below. Visitors walk upstairs and see the entire operation — that is the pitch moment.
Power Architecture

P-001 — Power Architecture

Bloom SOFC DC-Direct  |  No Solar  |  800V DC Native
Bloom SOFC is the only generation source — ~22 units, ~5,500 kW, intentionally overbuilt. Excess capacity is a feature: available for adjacent loads, future expansion, or sell-back. Natural gas to DC-direct at 54% efficiency — no inverter, no utility interconnect approval required. LFP battery on the 800V bus handles ATS bridging and the window between Bloom delivery and first power. Cat genset is emergency-only, AC output through a rectifier to the 800V bus. No solar — trees and shade make it a non-factor. Eaton handles facility-level distribution. Delta handles rack-level power. OCP Stage 1d throughout.
Cooling

C-001 — Cooling Flow

Bloom Exhaust → Munters → Free Dehumidification
Two independent chains. Top: Bloom SOFC 900°F exhaust feeds Munters HCD desiccant regeneration directly — Lafayette humidity problem solved at zero additional energy cost. Bottom: BAC adiabatic coolers (exterior) → CoolIT CHx200 CDU pairs → Staubli UQD quick-connects → Boyd cold plates + Danfoss CoolTrain valve trains inside the NVL72 racks. 45°C supply, 55-65°C return. Same cooling chain runs to all 3 exterior pods via insulated supply/return headers. PUE target 1.04–1.08.
