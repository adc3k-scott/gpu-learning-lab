S-001 — Site Overview

0.60 Acres  |  3 Parcels
0.60 acres, 3 parcels, owned outright. Building houses Floor 1 compute (10 NVL72 racks) and Floor 2 NOC/Command Hub. Three ADC 3K pods side by side on the adjacent concrete pad — each a 40-ft ISO container with 10 rack positions (8 compute + 1 network + 1 storage). Bloom SOFC cluster on remaining pad space — quiet, no combustion exhaust, no standoff distance required. Gas line: short run to main tap, same infrastructure corridor as Trappeys 0.5 mi east.

P-001 — Power Architecture

800V DC · OCP Stage 1d · Bloom SOFC Primary
Bloom SOFC is the primary generation source — 40 units, 10,000 kW. Sized for 40 racks × 130 kW = 5,200 kW IT, ~5,720 kW total facility at PUE 1.10 — 4,280 kW headroom. LFP battery bridges ATS. Cat genset emergency-only. Solar offset: 300 kW First Solar TR1 on pod roofs + Chag Street ground mount — no solar on main building roof (trees). Eaton = facility distribution. Delta = rack power. OCP Stage 1d throughout.
Cooling

C-001 — Cooling Flow

Bloom Exhaust → Munters → Free Dehumidification
Two independent chains. Top: Bloom SOFC 900°F exhaust feeds Munters HCD desiccant regeneration directly — Lafayette humidity problem solved at zero additional energy cost. Bottom: BAC adiabatic coolers → CoolIT CHx200 CDU pairs → Staubli UQD quick-connects → Boyd cold plates + Danfoss CoolTrain valve trains inside the NVL72 racks. 45°C supply, 55-65°C return. Same chain runs to all 3 exterior pods. PUE target 1.10.
