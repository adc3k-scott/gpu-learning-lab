Walk the Campus

The Trappey's Cannery site on the Vermilion River in Lafayette. 22 acres, 12 nationally registered structures, operating as a cannery from 1930 into the 1990s. Being restored and repurposed as Trappey's Compute Center — Louisiana's anchor AI compute facility at 91.1 MW Stage 1 IT load.
Overview

The Full Campus

22 acres on the Vermilion River between SE Evangeline Thruway and Guidry Street. 12 contributing structures from the original cannery operations (1930–1990s). Compute Halls — B3 & B4

Open-Span Industrial Envelope

The metal warehouse interiors become compute halls B3 and B4 — exactly what cassette-based compute needs: open floor plates with steel column structure, high ceilings for overhead cable and piping, industrial-grade concrete slab floors rated for cassette deployment. Cassettes arrive on flatbed trucks, drop on pre-poured pads inside the envelope, and connect through a single panel interface to the block switchboard.
Compute HallB3 — 20 CassettesSteel column grid on concrete slab. High ceiling accommodates overhead cassette cable trays and CoolIT CHx2000 CDU piping. 20 cassettes arranged in 5 blocks of 4 = 41,400 kW IT load.
Compute HallB4 — 24 CassettesLarger footprint accommodates 24 cassettes in 6 blocks of 4 = 49,680 kW IT load. Combined Stage 1 IT load across B3 + B4 = 91.1 MW.
B1 & B2 — Partner Hub + NOC

The Historic Brick Buildings

Buildings 1 and 2 are the original brick cannery structures — nationally registered, restored under Historic Tax Credit standards. They become the Network Operations Center (NOC), partner hub offices, community space, riverwalk access, and the campus welcome center. Rooftops carry the 2.05 MW First Solar array (3,731 panels total across both buildings). The 45% combined HTC stack (20% Federal + 25% Louisiana) applies to certified rehabilitation of these structures.

Rear Slab — Generation + Storage

42,000 Sq Ft, Sized for Full Build

The rear slab was sized from day one for the Full Build scenario (88 gensets at 182.2 MW). Stage 1 deploys 44 Cat CG260-16 natural gas gensets grouped by 11 blocks, 11 block transformers (13.8 kV → 480 VAC), 11 block switchboards, and 39.6 MWh of block-level LFP BESS. Jacket-water radiators reject ~58 MW of genset cooling to atmosphere on the same slab. LUS Pinhook Substation is adjacent — spatial allocation only, future optionality, not day-one dependency.

Infrastructure Yard — Cooling

28,000 Sq Ft — Cooling Tower Field

The infrastructure yard holds the cooling tower field that rejects GPU heat to atmosphere — ~72.9 MW at Stage 1, doubling to ~145.7 MW at Full Build. Wet mechanical draft cooling towers are the working basis, sized for Lafayette's 28°C ASHRAE 0.4% design wet-bulb. The SCR emissions-control units, facility circulation pumps, and make-up water treatment equipment share the yard. CoolIT CHx2000 CDU skids live adjacent to their respective cassettes in the compute halls, not in the yard.
Infrastructure Yard28,000 Sq Ft Concrete PadThe existing concrete pad that becomes the infrastructure yard. 6 wet mechanical draft cooling tower cells, SCR units, facility pumps, make-up water treatment.

Site Amenity

Vermilion River Frontage

The campus sits directly on the Vermilion River. The river is a site amenity: rebuilt seawall, public riverwalk, the defining photograph of the campus. It is not a thermal sink — Gulf Coast surface water peaks at 30–33°C in summer and the Vermilion is tidally influenced at Lafayette, so river cooling was eliminated from the engineering architecture. Heat rejection lives in the cooling tower field, not in the river.

History

Industrial Heritage

Six decades of canning operations at Trappey's. The cannery closed in the early 1990s and the buildings sat empty for 30+ years — the industrial envelope survived. 12 contributing structures, all National Register eligible. Restoration qualifies for the 45% HTC stack (20% Federal + 25% Louisiana).
