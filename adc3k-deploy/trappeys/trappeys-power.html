Power Architecture — From Gas Supply to GPU

Behind-the-meter permanent electrical island, day one. 44 Cat CG260-16 natural gas gensets organized into 11 replicated blocks. Each block converges on a 480 VAC switchboard; 800 VDC exists only inside each cassette, right where the GPUs need it. No grid. No sell-back. No interconnect queue.

One Block, End to End

A single block: four gensets paralleled at 13.8 kV, one step-down transformer, one 480 VAC switchboard as the convergence point, four cassette feeds, and AC-coupled BESS and solar on the same bus. Inside each cassette, Delta in-row power racks rectify to 800 VDC to feed the compute racks. 11 of these blocks make Stage 1.

Inside One Cassette — the 800 VDC Domain

The 480 VAC primary feed enters the cassette through an Eaton Magnum DS 6,000 A disconnect. Five Delta 660 kW in-row power racks rectify to the 800 VDC cassette-internal busway. That busway feeds nine Vera Rubin NVL72/CPX compute racks and one control rack. Rack-integrated capacitance and BBU absorb sub-100 ms GPU transients so the block BESS never has to.

Eaton 800 VDC Reference Design — Alignment Map

Eaton and NVIDIA co-presented a five-layer data center power architecture at GTC 2026. Our replicated-block deployment maps to all five layers; layers 1–3 reflect the Trappey's block topology, 4 and 5 cover cooling and software that live in separate documents.

Layer 1 — Generation & Grid
On-Site Generation, No Grid

44 Cat CG260-16 natural gas gensets (11 blocks × 4), First Solar 2.05 MW rooftop array, block-level BESS. LUS grid interconnect is future optionality only — not a generation layer day one.

Layer 2 — Gray Space
MV → LV Conditioning

Per-block step-down transformer (13.8 kV → 480 V, ~15 MVA), arc-resistant MV switchgear, 480 VAC main switchboard with solidly-grounded wye secondary and LSIG mains. Standard LV protection gear — no SSTs, no DCRPP skid.

Layer 3 — White Space
Cassette-Internal 800 VDC

480 VAC primary feed enters each cassette, gets rectified by five Delta 660 kW in-row power racks to an 800 VDC cassette-internal busway. The 800 VDC rail never leaves the cassette envelope.

Layer 4 — Cooling
Direct-to-Chip Liquid

CoolIT CHx2000 external CDU per cassette, facility plate HX (PHX-001), cooling tower field rejecting ~72.9 MW at Stage 1. Details on the Technology page.

Layer 5 — Software & Services
AMCL Five-Tier Control

L0 field devices → L1 block PLC (deterministic) → L2 plant SCADA → L3 AI dispatch → L4 HMI + IEC 62443 SL 2 cybersecurity. AI vendor selection is open.

Protection Philosophy — Island-Only, Block-Internal

No anti-islanding. No external sync. No grid reverse-power coordination. Protection coordinates inside each block, end of story. The block is the blast-radius boundary.

Stage 1 operates at 61.5% anchor loading across all 44 gensets — positive load margin of 2 to 4 MW at the campus level. AI dispatch envelope is 55–75%; 63–65% expected average. N-1 contingency at the block level (3 of 4 gensets carrying load) runs around 76% loading per surviving unit.

BESS — Stabilizer, Not Standby

The BESS is always active on the 480 VAC block bus — it runs continuously in charge/discharge dispatch under AMCL control, not waiting idle for a fault. LFP chemistry. ~3.6 MWh per block working, ~39.6 MWh Stage 1. Four operating functions in priority order: transient buffer, contingency support, solar clip recapture, and load shifting within the 55–75% AI dispatch envelope.

The BESS is not sized for extended outage. 15–20 minutes of graceful shutdown is sufficient for GPU checkpointing and orderly cooldown. The primary reliability path is gas supply continuity; the BESS buys time to shut down cleanly if gas is lost. AC-coupled through a Hitachi AMPS PCS SiC bidirectional inverter. Battery-pack vendor (Fluence, LG Energy Solution Vertech, Saft) selected via RFQ; Hitachi engaged on PCS layer regardless.
