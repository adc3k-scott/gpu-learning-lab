Zero GPU Infra

Walking Distance

Half a Mile from Campus

Trappeys sits 0.5 miles from UL Lafayette. NVIDIA research compute within walking distance. No network latency debates. No remote access friction. Students and faculty can physically work at the facility.

Value Proposition

What UL Lafayette Gets

This is not a one-sided ask. UL Lafayette gains a competitive research advantage that would cost $50M+ to build independently.

  • > Anchor Tenant Revenue. Built-in research compute demand from Day 1. Stable, predictable revenue stream for Trappeys. UL gets below-market GPU access in return.
  • > Federal Grant Competitiveness. NSF and DOE reviewers check for dedicated compute facilities. A GPU cluster half a mile from campus is a differentiator that most competing universities cannot match.
  • > Workforce Pipeline. Co-ops, internships, and direct employment pathways for CS, EE, and data science students. Real GPU operations experience, not classroom simulations.
  • > Historic Preservation Programs. Architecture and historic preservation students gain a real-world adaptive reuse project. Trappeys Cannery is a Louisiana landmark — documenting and contributing to its transformation is publishable academic work.
  • > City Credibility. Every stakeholder in this project — city council, LEDA, NVIDIA, investors — moves faster when the university is at the table. UL Lafayette's involvement signals institutional validation that no amount of marketing can replicate.
