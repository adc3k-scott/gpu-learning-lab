The Biggest Rocket Ever Flown

Confirmed

On May 22, 2026, Starship V3 flew for the first time — Flight 12, from the new Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas. It is the largest, most powerful launch vehicle ever built, and the footage is real and public.

What actually happened on Flight 12

First flight of the V3 vehicle and the first launch from Starbase Pad 2. The booster's redesigned fuel transfer line feeding its 33 engines is roughly the size of an entire Falcon 9 first stage. The ship deployed 20 mock Starlink satellites and flew an hour-long suborbital arc halfway around the world to the Indian Ocean, splashing down upright before toppling and igniting — an outcome SpaceX expected for an ocean landing. The booster lost engines on its boostback attempt and crash-landed downrange. Net result: the new vehicle, new engines, new pad, and payload deploy all flew on the first try.

The Data Center Leaves Earth

Announced · specs published

Days before the IPO, SpaceX published the design of AI1 — its first orbital data center satellite. The pitch: in orbit you get continuous solar power, no water, no grid interconnect queue, and a vacuum to radiate heat into. The plan is a constellation that could eventually reach terawatt scale.
