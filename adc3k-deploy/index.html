01 — COOLING

Why immersion cooling changes everything.

Modern AI chips generate extreme thermal density. A single AI GPU produces heat equivalent to a kitchen toaster, concentrated into a chip the size of your hand. Air cooling physically cannot remove heat fast enough at these power densities.

Immersion cooling submerges the entire server in engineered dielectric fluid that conducts heat 1,200x more efficiently than air. No fans. No HVAC. No chillers. No water.

Traditional Air Cooling

The Old Way

Computer room air conditioners. PUE 1.4-1.6 — 40-60% of power wasted on cooling. Raised floors, hot/cold aisle containment, massive HVAC. Fails above ~1 kW per rack density.

ADC 3K Immersion

The New Way

Servers submerged in EC-110 dielectric fluid. PUE 1.05 — only 5% overhead. No fans, no HVAC, no water. Operates at 100 kW+ per rack. Quiet. Zero water consumption.

What Is an AI Factory?

You've probably heard the term but never needed to think about it. This page explains AI factories in plain language — what they are, why they matter, and why they're coming to Louisiana.

01 — THE SHORT ANSWER

An AI factory is a specialized facility built to run artificial intelligence.

A AI factory is a specialized facility filled with powerful computers called servers that run 24/7, doing work that keeps the modern world running.

Every time you open your phone, check email, watch Netflix, use GPS, ask an AI a question, or pay with a credit card — your request travels to a server in a AI factory, gets processed, and comes back in milliseconds.

Think of it like a power plant, but for information. A power plant takes fuel and turns it into electricity that flows to every home. A AI factory takes electricity and turns it into computing power that flows to every device. Without AI factories, there is no internet, no AI, no digital economy.

04 — THE HEAT PROBLEM

The #1 problem is heat.

AI chips generate extreme heat — as much as a kitchen toaster per chip. Traditional AI factories use air conditioning, but AI chips are now so powerful that air can't remove heat fast enough. That's where immersion cooling comes in — and that's what the ADC 3K pod uses.

05 — OLD WAY vs. NEW WAY

The AI factory industry is changing.

Traditional

The Old Way

Giant warehouse-sized building. Custom every time. 18-36 months to build. $500M+. Air cooled. A few major markets only.

ADC 3K Pod

The New Way

Factory-built inside a shipping container. Manufactured in Lafayette. Immersion cooled. Truck-shippable. Concrete pad + power + fiber = operational in weeks.

06 — WHAT DOES "EDGE" MEAN

Edge = closer to where the work happens.

Most AI factories are concentrated in a handful of places. Your data travels 1,000+ miles to get processed. Edge AI factories bring computing power closer — right to the factory, the oil field, the city.

Think of it like electricity. Power plants generate electricity, but you need substations near your neighborhood to deliver it. Edge AI factories are the substations of the digital world — bringing computing power closer to where it's needed.

The Gulf Coast is a perfect location for edge AI. Oil & gas, petrochemical, ports, energy — all need high-performance computing nearby, not 1,000 miles away.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this like a bitcoin mining operation?
No. Bitcoin mining uses specialized hardware for a single purpose. The ADC 3K pod houses general-purpose AI GPUs — the same technology powering ChatGPT, medical imaging, autonomous vehicles, and defense systems. The hardware is far more sophisticated and the applications create real economic value across every industry.
How loud is it?
Remarkably quiet. Immersion cooling eliminates all server fans inside the container. The outdoor dry coolers produce sound similar to commercial HVAC. Far quieter than traditional air-cooled AI factories.
How much water does it use?
Zero. The ADC 3K pod uses dry coolers instead of evaporative cooling towers. Traditional AI factories consume millions of gallons per year. This is an environmental and operational advantage.
How much power does it need?
3.0 MW-class — roughly equivalent to 2,000-2,500 homes. Requires a medium-voltage utility feed or generator interconnect. Initial deployments may operate at lower capacity and scale up to full 3.0 MW.
Is the cooling liquid dangerous?
No. EC-110 engineered dielectric fluid is non-toxic, non-conductive, and non-flammable. Safe for human contact. Environmentally friendly. Recyclable at end of life. No HAZMAT classification required.
Who controls the data?
You do. Unlike cloud services, the ADC 3K pod is a privately owned asset at your location. Data never leaves unless you send it. Critical for defense, healthcare, oil & gas, and government where data sovereignty isn't optional.
Can it survive a hurricane?
The ADC 3K pod is built inside an ISO shipping container — one of the most rugged structures ever designed. Our 4-layer power architecture was specifically built around Hurricane Ida lessons: Layer 2 diesel keeps us running independently of both the grid and the nat gas pipeline, which was disrupted for weeks during Ida.
Does it need building permits?
Classified as equipment, not a building. In most jurisdictions, equipment placement requires utility permits and site approval but not traditional building permits. Requirements vary by parish — we navigate the permitting process for each deployment.
What kind of investment opportunity is this?
AI compute infrastructure is generating strong infrastructure-grade returns in markets where it's been deployed. We're building that market in Louisiana. Contact us for investor materials — we don't publish financial projections publicly, but we walk qualified investors through the full financial model.
