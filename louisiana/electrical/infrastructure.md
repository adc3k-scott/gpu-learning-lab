# Louisiana Electrical Infrastructure — ADC Lafayette Sites
Last updated: 2026-03-25

## LUS (Lafayette Utilities System) — Municipal, NOT State Regulated
- All 3 ADC sites in LUS territory (Trappeys, MARLIE, KLFT)
- Entergy has ~100 customers in Lafayette Parish (virtually zero presence)
- SLEMCO serves unincorporated areas outside city limits
- LUS is MUNICIPAL — rates/interconnection decided by CITY COUNCIL, not LPSC
- This means ADC negotiates directly with local elected officials

## Rates — Schedule C2 (Large General Service)
- Customer charge: $50/mo
- Demand: $8.60/kW
- Energy: $0.02119/kWh + fuel adjustment
- All-in average: ~$0.12/kWh (38% below national average)
- BUT: ADC runs behind-the-meter, so actual grid purchases minimal

## Grid Capacity
- 18 substations at 69/13.8 kV
- ~505 MW system-wide peak demand
- Pin Hook substation near Trappeys (visible from site)
- ADC's 0.65-3 MW phased load = well within normal substation capacity
- Behind-the-meter = ADC is net-zero or net-NEGATIVE load on grid

## Net Metering
- LUS allows it
- Credits at avoided cost (~$0.03/kWh), NOT retail ($0.12/kWh)
- Self-consumption is 4x more valuable than sell-back
- Systems above 480V require case-by-case engineering review

## Underground Conversion — The I-49 Lever
- I-49 Lafayette Connector ($2B, 5.5 miles along Evangeline Thruway to airport)
- When it moves: ALL overhead utilities get relocated underground
- SEIS targeted end of 2025
- This solves the above-ground wiring problem WITHOUT ADC paying for it
