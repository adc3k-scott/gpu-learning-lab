Replicated-Block
Architecture

Trappey's Compute Center is 11 electrically independent blocks on a 480 VAC bus, each feeding four cassettes that host NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72/CPX compute. The cassette is the product IP. Everything outside it is vendor-neutral infrastructure. Behind-the-meter permanent island, day one.

Campus Topology

11 Replicated Blocks — Same Pattern, Different Instances

Stage 1 is 11 identical blocks. Each block is electrically self-contained — four gensets, one step-down transformer, one 480 VAC switchboard, four cassette feeds. No campus MV ring. No inter-block tie on day one. Contained blast radius and standardized procurement.

Electrical Architecture

480 VAC Block Bus, 800 VDC Cassette-Internal

Gensets parallel at 13.8 kV, step down to 480 VAC at the block switchboard, and feed four cassettes at 480 VAC. The 800 VDC rail exists only inside each cassette — where five Delta 660 kW in-row power racks do the rectification right next to the GPUs. BESS and solar are AC-coupled to the same 480 VAC bus. Standard low-voltage protection gear campus-wide.

Layer 1
Generation
Per block: 4 gensets
4 × Cat CG260-16 natural gas gensets paralleled at 13.8 kV via Cat ECS isochronous sharing. 4,000 ekW continuous per genset. 43.8% electrical efficiency. Design anchor 61.5% loading — three gensets cover the block; the fourth is N+1 for maintenance. <250 mg/Nm³ NOx at 5% O&sub2;. Hydrogen blending to 25% as future optionality.
Layer 2
Step-down
13.8 kV → 480 VAC
One block transformer per block — 13.8 kV Δ → 480 Y/277 V, ~15 MVA continuous, dry-type or cast-resin (no oil, lower fire load), Z ≈ 6%. Dedicated to the block with no cross-block sharing. 87T overall differential protection on the transformer. This is the one topology element that differs from the 5 MW Marlie reference design, whose G3520K generates 480 VAC directly without a step-down.
Layer 3
Block Bus
480 VAC switchboard
One 480 VAC switchboard per block — solidly grounded wye secondary, 50G residual ground sensing, LSIG main trip unit. This is the single convergence point for everything in the block: four cassette primary feeds, BESS inverter, solar PV inverter, cooling tower MCC, facility ancillary. Bus and main breaker sized to 17,000 A minimum to accommodate simultaneous BESS peak injection and solar.
Layer 4
Storage + Solar
AC-coupled at 480 VAC
BESS and solar live on the same 480 VAC bus. BESS (LFP, ~3.6 MWh per block, 39.6 MWh Stage 1) couples through a Hitachi AMPS PCS SiC bidirectional inverter — always active as a stabilizer, not standby. Solar (2.05 MW First Solar Series 7 CdTe on B1/B2 rooftops) couples through a grid-forming PV inverter. Sub-cycle transient response via SiC; block-wide sub-100 ms GPU events are absorbed inside each cassette.
Layer 5
Cassette
800 VDC internal
Inside each cassette — 480 VAC feed enters through an Eaton Magnum DS 6,000 A main disconnect. Five Delta 660 kW in-row power racks rectify to an 800 VDC cassette-internal busway. That bus feeds nine Vera Rubin NVL72/CPX compute racks and one control rack. Rack-integrated BBU and Power Capacitance Shelf handle sub-100 ms GPU load transients. The 800 VDC rail never leaves the cassette envelope.
Why 480 VAC at block level: standard LV distribution and protection gear is mature, well-supplied, and vendor-competitive. Routing 800 VDC across the campus is not required — the GPUs benefit from 800 VDC inside the cassette where the conversion-distance advantage applies. This keeps procurement simple outside, performance tight inside.

Thermal Architecture

Two Independent Heat Paths

GPU heat takes one path: direct-to-chip liquid from the CoolIT CHx2000 skid through a plate heat exchanger to a cooling tower field. Genset waste heat takes an independent path: exhaust slip-stream to Munters desiccant regeneration, remainder to stack; jacket water to dedicated air-cooled radiators. The Vermilion River is a site amenity, not a thermal loop.

GPU Heat Path — Cassette to Cooling Tower

NVIDIA ships every NVL72/CPX compute rack with a direct-to-chip liquid loop at the die. The CoolIT CHx2000 external CDU skid loops that warm water through the cassette and returns it to the facility at ~50–55°C. A plate heat exchanger (PHX-001) sits at the facility boundary, isolating the closed CDU circuit from the open cooling tower circuit. Cooling towers in the 28,000 sq ft infrastructure yard reject ~72.9 MW at Stage 1 (~145.7 MW at Full Build) to atmosphere. Wet mechanical draft is the working basis — Lafayette's 28°C ASHRAE 0.4% design wet-bulb gives a tower supply around 31°C at a 3°C approach, comfortably within the ≤45°C GPU supply requirement.

Genset Waste Heat — Independent Path

Genset waste heat never mixes with the GPU cooling loop. The Cat CG260-16 exhaust is split two ways: a 125 kW slip-stream per cassette drives the Munters DSS Pro desiccant regeneration (5.5 MW Stage 1, 11 MW Full Build), and the remainder vents through the stack. All jacket water (~58 MW at Stage 1, 61.5% load) rejects through dedicated air-cooled radiators on the rear slab. No heat recovery unit. No absorption chiller. No chilled-water plant. The architecture was deliberately simplified in April 2026 to eliminate the LiBr absorption chiller and its supporting systems.

Dedicated air-cooled radiators on the rear slab reject jacket-water heat directly to atmosphere. No coupling to the GPU cooling loop.
The Vermilion River is not a thermal sink. Gulf Coast surface water peaks at 30–33°C in summer and the river is tidally influenced at Lafayette. The river remains a site amenity — rebuilt seawall, riverwalk, the defining photograph of the campus — but it carries no process heat. The historic water tower is preserved as a site landmark; it has no role in the cooling system.

Control Architecture

AMCL — Five-Tier AI Mission Control Layer

AI dispatch is the operating model, not a decoration. The AMCL stack is five tiers with strict separation: protection is deterministic and local; the AI sits above the protection layer and orchestrates generation, thermal, and storage within the protection envelope — it cannot override it.

L0 — Field Devices

Sensor and Actuator Layer

Cat ECS governors, generator protection IEDs, VFDs on all thermal auxiliaries, Jetson AGX Orin cassette BMS (148 channels per cassette), RTDs, CTs/PTs, cooling-tower instrumentation, BESS BMS.

L1 — Block PLC

Deterministic, Local

Genset paralleling, three-stage UFLS, BESS PCS setpoints, MPPT coordination, protection trip schemes, safety interlocks. Cannot be overridden from higher tiers.

Deterministic — cannot be overridden

L2 — Plant SCADA

Data and Historian

OPC-UA backbone. Alarm management. Cassette BMS aggregation: 148 channels × 44 cassettes = ~6,500 cassette points plus facility points. Stage 1 scale, scales directly to Full Build.

L3 — AMCL AI Dispatch

Optimization Layer

Cross-block optimization against IT load, thermal profile, BESS state, solar production, and gas supply. Runs the 55–75% genset loading envelope. Orchestrates BESS across four operating functions. Does not override protection.

Generation · thermal · storage · solar

L4 — HMI + Cybersecurity

Operator + IEC 62443

Operator override, HMI, IEC 62443 SL 2 segmentation, OT/IT isolation (important for colocation customers), incident response, admin plane hardening, supply-chain integrity for control equipment and model weights.

AI Failure Mode

Last-Known-Good

If the AI dispatch layer fails or is isolated, the facility reverts to last-known-good deterministic control. Gensets hold setpoint, BESS PCS holds mode, VFDs hold speed, governors and protection run autonomously. Operations takes manual dispatch until AI is restored.

AI vendor selection is open. AMCL is the architectural pattern; the implementing vendor for the L3 layer (model provider, dispatch engine, orchestration platform) is not yet selected. The L0–L2 field / PLC / SCADA layers operate autonomously regardless of L3 vendor choice.
