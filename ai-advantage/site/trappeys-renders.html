Restoration Renderings

Kontext edits on real site photographs. Same buildings, restored and maintained. National Register rehabilitation standards preserve the historic character of B1, B2, the water tower, and the industrial envelope while the compute halls (B3, B4), rear slab generation plant, and infrastructure-yard cooling plant are deployed.
Before / After — Restoration Vision

Riverfront — The Hero Shot

Before — Abandoned 30+ Years
After — Restored + Rooftop Solar
B1 and B2 historic brick buildings restored under SHPO-approved rehabilitation standards. Rooftop hosts 2.05 MW of First Solar Series 7 CdTe (3,731 panels across both rooftops). The water tower is restored and preserved as a site landmark.

Riverfront — Wide View

Before — From the Bayou
After — Seawall Restored
Rebuilt seawall and public riverwalk along the Vermilion River. The river is preserved as a site amenity — not a thermal sink. Heat rejection lives in the cooling tower field in the infrastructure yard, not in the river.

Water Tower + Warehouse

Before — Debris, Graffiti, Decay
After — Cleaned, Solar, Landscaped
The iconic water tower — sandblasted, primed with industrial zinc, repainted in Trappey's heritage livery. Restored as a site landmark only; no role in the thermal system.

Compute Hall Interior (B3 / B4)

Before — Empty Warehouse
After — Cassette Deployment
Open-span metal-warehouse envelope restored as a compute hall. 40-foot cassettes on pre-poured pads, each cassette holding 9 Vera Rubin NVL72/CPX compute racks, 1 control rack, and 5 Delta 660 kW in-row power racks. CoolIT CHx2000 external CDU skids sit adjacent to each cassette.

Street Entrance + Loading Docks

Before — Abandoned Entrance
After — Active Campus
Original loading docks become the main partner-hub entry for B1. Historic brick facade preserved; original arched bays and roll-up doors retained as architectural features.
