Critical Path & Risk Notes

  • Critical path: Omniverse design (8 wk) → Foundation (4 wk) → Steel erection (6 wk) → Genset install (3 wk) → Robot install (4 wk) → Commissioning (4 wk) = 29 weeks sequential + overlaps = ~14 months
  • Robot lead time: Fanuc and ABB robots have 12-16 week lead times. POs MUST be placed during pre-construction phase to arrive when building is ready.
  • ITEP filing: Must be filed BEFORE any groundbreaking. Missing this deadline forfeits 80% property tax abatement for 10 years ($500K+ NPV).
  • Genset delivery: CAT G3516H generators have 16-20 week lead time. Order with robots in pre-construction.
  • Weather risk: Louisiana hurricane season (Jun-Nov) overlaps with Phases 2-3. Metal building erection should avoid peak season if possible — target foundation pour before June.
  • PEMB lead time: Nucor/BlueScope metal buildings: 10-14 week fabrication + delivery. Order during permitting phase.
  • Parallel work: Solar roof, gas pipeline tap, and interior MEP all happen concurrently with structure — no float impact.
  • Omniverse advantage: Digital twin starts in pre-construction and continues through commissioning. Robot paths programmed in Isaac Sim BEFORE robots are installed — zero rework on first power-up.
  • Baton Rouge Terminal (Step 1): Operating and generating revenue during entire 14-month factory construction. Cash flow covers part of construction costs.
