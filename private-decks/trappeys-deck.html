Energy Architecture
Slide 9 of 18
4-Layer Power Hierarchy

We Make Our
Own Power.

Redundant by design. Hurricane Ida took the grid and the pipelines for weeks. Our architecture accounts for both failure modes — independently.

Layer 1 — Solar (PRIMARY OFFSET)
2.05 MW rooftop — 3,731 First Solar Series 7 TR1 panels across 112,500 sq ft. Made 30 miles away in New Iberia. 800 VDC direct-coupled to DSX bus at 97% efficiency.
Layer 2 — Natural Gas (BACKBONE)
Carries the main load 24/7. ALWAYS running. Henry Hub pricing — 40 miles away. ATMOS Energy gas hub confirmed at Pinhook, trunk lines through buildings. Cloudy day = gas carries full load.
Layer 3 — Diesel Gensets (EMERGENCY)
On-site fuel storage. Pipeline-independent. Ida-proven. Activates when gas supply is compromised — hurricane-rated emergency power.
Layer 4 — Grid / LUS (SELL-BACK ONLY)
Excess power goes BACK to the grid. NOT a consumption source. LUS Pin Hook Substation right next door. Revenue stream, not a dependency.

Cooling Strategy
Slide 10 of 18
The Vermilion River Advantage

The River Is
Our Cooling System.
No HVAC. No Chillers.

The Vermilion River runs alongside the Trappeys site — providing a natural free-cooling resource that eliminates the largest power draw in traditional facilities. Combined with NVIDIA's integrated liquid cooling, this site achieves PUE levels that air-cooled facilities can't touch.

◉
River water heat exchange — free cooling baseline
Leverages the Vermilion River's thermal mass for primary heat rejection. No chillers. No compressors. Massive energy savings.
⬡
NVIDIA integrated liquid cooling — highest GPU density possible
NVIDIA ships complete liquid-cooled racks (45°C hot water, 2-hour install). No hot aisle/cold aisle. Rack-level thermal management built in.
◈
PUE target: 1.03 — best-in-class efficiency
Industry average is 1.58. At 1.03, nearly every watt goes to compute — not cooling overhead. Dramatically better unit economics.
▦
No HVAC buildout — lower CapEx, faster deployment
Traditional facility HVAC is 30-40% of construction cost. We skip it entirely. The river does the work.

Investment Structure

Multiple Capital

for Risk Reduction.

Historic tax credits, state incentives, and private equity combine to reduce effective CapEx below what any greenfield site could achieve. The building's heritage is a financial asset.

Historic Tax Credits — 20% Federal + 25% State
Certified historic structure. Federal 20% rehab credit + Louisiana 25% state credit. Combined: 45% of qualified rehab costs returned as tax credits.
Act 730 + PILOT — Operating Incentives
Sales tax exemption on data center equipment + parish PILOT agreement on property taxes. Ongoing cost reduction that compounds over the investment horizon.
Private Equity + Debt — Senior Secured
Institutional equity raise + senior secured debt against the real asset. Building provides hard collateral. Tenant LOIs provide revenue certainty for lenders.
