Thermal Architecture

Boyd Corporation's thermal division — acquired by Eaton for $9.5 billion in March 2026 — supplies the ROL2300-48U40 row-level CDU: 2,300 kW capacity at 8°C ATD, N+1 redundant seal-less pumps, 30.6+ billion field hours with zero leaks. Humidity Control
Munters Desiccant — No AC. Anywhere.

Traditional facilities burn 30–50% of total power on cooling. This cassette burns less than 5%. The Munters ICA with RightDry technology uses waste heat from the CDU reject loop — already at 85–95°C — to regenerate the desiccant rotor. The thermodynamic loop closes on itself: GPU heat → coolant → CDU → hot water reject → Munters regeneration → dry air back into the cassette. No case study of desiccant-only liquid-cooled deployment exists. This is a new category.

If it holds PUE ≤ 1.05 in Lafayette in August at 95°F / 80% RH — it works anywhere on the planet.
Lafayette's Gulf Coast summer conditions are the hardest humidity environment in the continental US. The POC site is not just a build location — it is a climate stress chamber. Every competitor in Oregon or Scandinavia leans on cold ambient air. This cassette needs none of it.
Core Innovation — CHP Cascade Architecture

Combined Heat and Power cascade is the technology that makes PUE ≤ 1.05 structurally achievable — not a design target. The Cat G3520K or Bloom Energy SOFC produces electricity for compute and simultaneously generates 1,372 kW of recoverable jacket water heat (99°C) and 1,128 kW of exhaust heat (397°C). Total system efficiency: 88.7%. That waste heat — instead of being rejected into the atmosphere — cascades into two systems that would otherwise draw from the electrical load:

System 1 — Munters Desiccant Regeneration
Humidity Control Off the Power Bill

The Munters ICA desiccant rotor requires heat to regenerate — normally a significant electrical parasitic load. In the CHP cascade, exhaust heat from the genset or SOFC drives the regeneration cycle directly. The Munters draws nothing from the compute power budget. Dehumidification is thermodynamically free.

System 2 — CDU Secondary Cooling Loop
Chip-to-Chip Cooling Driven by Waste Heat

The CDU secondary loop — the circuit that carries heat directly away from the GPU chips — is driven by the CHP jacket water circuit. The thermal cascade does the pumping work that would otherwise require dedicated electrical infrastructure. Both parasitic loads eliminated. That is how the cassette achieves PUE ≤ 1.05 in the worst humidity environment in the continental US.

This architecture is power-source agnostic. Cat G3520K genset during POC phase. Bloom Energy SOFC at scale. FuelCell Energy MCFC as second qualified platform. The CHP cascade works identically regardless of heat source — the thermodynamic loop closes on waste heat, not on the fuel type that created it.

Worst-Case Validation Protocol
  • Boyd Rack Emulators (2×, 250 kW each) — NVIDIA-collaborative thermal load simulators. Validate liquid cooling infrastructure before a single GPU is installed. Permanent test infrastructure — every future cassette is validated against them.
  • Reference-grade instrumentation independent of the BMS — Vaisala HMT370 humidity/temperature transmitters and DMT152 dew point sensors at five cassette positions. NIST-traceable. Continuous timestamped data stream. Two independent systems, same conclusion.
  • Atmospheric stress injection — Nortec or DriSteem industrial humidifier forces intake conditions to 100°F / 95% RH. More extreme than any recorded Lafayette weather event.
  • The deliverable: a single Worst-Case Validation Report that goes in front of every investor, every customer, and every NVIDIA reviewer. PUE under manufactured worst-case conditions. That document is the most valuable piece of paper in the investor package.
