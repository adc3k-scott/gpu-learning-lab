ADC · MISSION CONTROL The Pure DC Principle 02 / 08 CONFIDENTIAL
Why Pure DC. Why Now.
The 100-Year AC Assumption
Is Broken.
For a century, generation was AC because rotating machines spin and output AC. Every downstream system was built around that physical fact. That assumption no longer holds.
The old reason AC existed
Rotating generators spin and output AC. Distribution at high AC voltage was efficient. Transformation was cheap. Everything downstream adapted to AC.
Fuel cells generate DC electrochemically
Solid oxide and molten carbonate fuel cells have no spinning parts. They produce DC at the electrode. There is no physical reason to convert to AC — that step only exists if you inherit AC infrastructure.
Solar panels output DC natively
First Solar TR1 panels output DC. BESS stores and discharges DC. The GPU chip needs DC. Every conversion between source and chip costs efficiency, capital, and failure surface.
OCP Stage 1d validates 1500V DC distribution
Published March 2026. The Open Compute Project ratified 1500V DC as the standard for AI factory distribution. This is not experimental — it is the published industry spec.
NVIDIA DSX requires 800V DC at the rack
The NVIDIA Data Center Scale X program mandates 800V DC delivery to the rack sidecar. We designed to that spec and extended forward to 1500V DC — no retrofit when the industry moves.
Pure DC eliminates every unnecessary conversion
Inverters, rectifiers, UPS rooms, transformer banks, synchronization gear, and grid interconnect all exist only because AC used to be unavoidable. Remove the AC assumption and they disappear.

No AC generation permitted at any point in the generation or distribution chain. Every source ties directly into the DC bus.

Distribution design must conform to OCP DCF LVDC v1.0. 1500V DC ceiling. Stage 1d grounding and protection requirements apply.
NFPA 855 (BESS only)
Battery energy storage systems must be NFPA 855 primary, UL 9540A tested, FM Global Outdoor Class 1.
NEC Article 712
All DC system wiring, grounding, and protection must comply with NEC Article 712 DC systems requirements.
NVIDIA DSX compatible at rack interface
Power delivery to rack must be compatible with Eaton ORV3 sidecar and NVIDIA NVL72 platform at 800V DC, 130 kW per rack.
