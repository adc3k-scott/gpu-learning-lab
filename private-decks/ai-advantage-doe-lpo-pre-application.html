Section 01
Project Overview

AI Advantage requests consideration from the Department of Energy Loan Programs Office under Title XVII Section 1703 for financing of the MARLIE I AI compute facility in Lafayette, Louisiana — the first deployment of the AI Advantage Pure DC AI Cassette and CHP Cascade Architecture.

The project solves a fundamental problem in artificial intelligence infrastructure: power and cooling inefficiency. The global AI compute industry operates at an average Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) of 1.58, meaning that for every watt delivered to a GPU, 0.58 additional watts are consumed by cooling, power conversion, and facility overhead. In hot, humid climates like the Gulf Coast, this figure is typically higher. AI Advantage eliminates this overhead through a Combined Heat and Power Cascade that recovers engine waste heat to drive all facility cooling — achieving PUE ≤ 1.05 at full load in Lafayette, Louisiana in August.

The facility operates entirely behind the meter with no grid connection and behind the valve with no gas utility billing — natural gas from a wellhead or pipeline upstream of the meter is the sole energy input. Power distribution is 800V DC native, compliant with Open Compute Project Stage 1d. No AC conversion anywhere in the power path.

Section 02
Innovative Technology Description

Project Name: AI Advantage Pure DC AI Cassette — MARLIE I Demonstration Facility
Location: Lafayette, Louisiana
Technology Category: Combined Heat and Power / Advanced Energy Efficiency / AI Infrastructure

AI Advantage has engineered a 2.3 MW artificial intelligence compute cassette that achieves a Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) of 1.05 or better under Gulf Coast summer conditions — 95°F ambient, 80% relative humidity — by eliminating all compressor-based cooling from the electrical bus entirely.

The core innovation is the CHP Cascade Architecture. A Caterpillar G3520H natural gas generator produces 2,507 kW of electrical output. Its exhaust gas, at 397°C, regenerates a Munters ICA desiccant rotor, which controls facility humidity without consuming a single watt of electricity. Its jacket water, at 99°C, drives a lithium bromide absorption chiller, which feeds the secondary loop of a Boyd/Eaton ROL2300-48U40 row-level CDU handling the full 2.3 MW cassette thermal load. The thermodynamic loop closes on itself. No compressors. No refrigerants. No cooling load on the electrical bus.

CHP Cascade Flow: Cat G3520H exhaust (397°C) → Munters ICA desiccant regeneration → humidity controlled, zero electrical draw. Jacket water (99°C) → LiBr absorption chiller → Boyd/Eaton CDU secondary loop → direct-to-chip cooling. Power path: natural gas → 2,507 kW electricity → 800V DC bus → NVIDIA NVL72 racks. One fuel input. Three outputs. Zero grid.

The facility operates entirely behind the meter with no grid connection and behind the valve with no gas utility — wellhead or pipeline gas upstream of the meter is the sole energy input. Power distribution is 800V DC native, compliant with Open Compute Project Stage 1d, with no AC conversion anywhere in the power path. This eliminates inverter and rectifier losses and is natively compatible with NVIDIA's NVL72 GPU architecture.

The cassette is contained in a standard 40-ft ISO High Cube container, engineered, permitted, and built by the founder — a licensed Louisiana general contractor. All 22 vendor partners are US-domiciled manufacturers.

No peer-reviewed case study exists for desiccant-only liquid-cooled AI compute deployment. AI Advantage is a new category. The technology is covered by a provisional patent across 13 claims filed by the applicant.

The system is power-source agnostic. The CHP Cascade operates identically whether the heat source is a Caterpillar G3520H natural gas genset (initial deployment), a Bloom Energy SOFC (scale deployment), or a FuelCell Energy MCFC (second qualified platform). The thermodynamic loop closes on waste heat, not on the fuel type that created it.

Lafayette, Louisiana in August — 95°F, 80% relative humidity — is the hardest humidity environment in the continental United States for compute infrastructure. Every competing operator relies on cold ambient air: Oregon, Scandinavia, the Pacific Northwest. AI Advantage requires none of it. If PUE ≤ 1.05 holds in Lafayette in August, it holds anywhere on the planet.
