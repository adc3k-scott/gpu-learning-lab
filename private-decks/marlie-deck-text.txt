        GROQ LPU — INFERENCE ACCELERATION

        
          Groq Language Processing Units (LPUs) are purpose-built inference engines —
          not trained on Groq hardware, but run on Groq hardware at speeds
          no GPU can match for deterministic single-stream inference workloads.

    02 — Government Funding Stack — Federal + State + Local
  

  

  

    
      Federal

      One Big Beautiful Bill Act

      
        Signed July 4, 2025, the OBBBA creates the most favorable federal policy environment for
        domestic AI infrastructure in US history. It codifies FEOC (Foreign Entity of Concern)
        restrictions — any project with Chinese, Russian, Iranian, or North Korean supply
        chain involvement is disqualified from federal tax credits, grants, and incentives. Marlie I
        is purpose-built for full compliance: NVIDIA hardware manufactured in Texas
        (Foxconn Houston, Wistron Fort Worth), 100% US ownership, Louisiana-licensed GC. The OBBBA
        also added $500M to the BEAD broadband program and raised Gulf OCS revenue
        sharing to $650M/year — both of which directly benefit Louisiana AI infrastructure.
        The $500B Stargate public-private AI program runs parallel to the OBBBA; Marlie I's FEOC-clean
        structure qualifies it for Stargate-aligned federal procurement and financing channels.
      

    

    
      Federal

      BEAD Broadband Program

      
        The Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) Program distributes federal
        funding to expand broadband infrastructure in underserved areas. Lafayette sits within a region
        with significant BEAD allocation. AI factories require massive bandwidth — and the presence of
        LUS Fiber, a city-owned gigabit network, creates a unique opportunity to layer
        BEAD-funded backhaul expansion directly into the Marlie I connectivity plan.
        LUS Fiber is on site at Marlie I — live and connected. The AI
        factory accelerates BEAD project eligibility for the City of Lafayette while simultaneously
        solving Marlie I's connectivity requirements.
      

    

    
      Federal

      Gulf OCS Revenue

      
        The OBBBA raised Louisiana's annual Gulf of America OCS revenue-sharing cap from
        $500M to $650M/year through 2034 — a 30% increase in the state's offshore
        energy dividend. Louisiana's share (~$200M/year at cap) funds the Coastal Master Plan,
        the infrastructure backbone that protects sites like Marlie I from long-term flood risk.
        Simultaneously, Louisiana's offshore energy production — the same natural gas supply chain
        feeding Marlie I's generators — is the economic engine behind this revenue. Henry Hub,
        the world's most liquid natural gas trading point, is 40 miles from Marlie I in Erath,
        LA. Marlie I's fuel costs are benchmarked to the most competitive gas price on earth.
      

    

    
      State

      Louisiana Act 730

      
        Louisiana Act 730 (effective July 1, 2024) provides a 20-year state and local
        sales and use tax rebate on qualifying AI factory equipment, software, construction
        materials, and installation — with a 10-year renewal option (30 years total). Qualification
        requires $200M+ in capital investment and 50+ new permanent jobs
        certified by Louisiana Economic Development. Marlie I's capital plan is structured to meet
        both thresholds. Once certified, a Direct Payment Number eliminates tax
        liability on the single largest cost in the project: GPU hardware. Stackable with the
        Quality Jobs Program (6% payroll rebate, 10 years) and LED FastStart
        (free customized workforce training). Combined with Louisiana's #1-ranked industrial
        electricity rates, Act 730 makes Marlie I's per-token economics unbeatable.
