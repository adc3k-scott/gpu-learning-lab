Why Inside Munters Beats Trappeys Approach

At Trappeys, Munters skids sit outside exposed to weather. At the Walmart, Munters drops inside the building shell directly adjacent to the hot aisle — weather protected, shorter duct runs, cleaner install. The existing 28–32 ft clear span handles airflow management without structural modification. Drop the retail ceiling, expose the clear span, add hot-aisle containment. Done.

How Marlie I Informs This Build

MARLIE I proved the sectioned-commissioning model — control the zone you're using, expand as racks come in. The Walmart scales that model by 20×. Start with ~5,000 SF and 50–100 racks. Commission the next section when rack supply arrives. You are never ahead of demand, never behind the building. MARLIE is limited by its walls. This site has no ceiling.

Strategic Position
The rack installation rate — not the building — is the bottleneck for every AI factory. This site absorbs whatever allocation comes in, in whatever phasing it arrives, without stranding capex. Start with one rack. Scale to 1,200+. The infrastructure grows with the demand.
