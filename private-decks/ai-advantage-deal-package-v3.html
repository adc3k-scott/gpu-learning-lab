Humidity Control
Munters Desiccant — No AC. Anywhere.

Traditional facilities burn 30–50% of total power on cooling. This cassette targets less than 5%. The Munters ICA with RightDry technology uses waste heat from the CHP system — exhaust gas at 397°C — to regenerate the desiccant rotor. The thermodynamic loop closes on itself: CHP exhaust heat → Munters regeneration → dry air back into the cassette. No case study of desiccant-only liquid-cooled deployment exists. This is a new category.

If it holds PUE ≤ 1.05 in Lafayette in August at 95°F / 80% RH — it works anywhere on the planet.
Lafayette's Gulf Coast summer conditions are the hardest humidity environment in the continental US. Every competitor in Oregon or Scandinavia leans on cold ambient air. This cassette needs none of it. Deploy it in Houston, the Permian Basin, the Middle East, or sub-Saharan Africa — the thermal architecture is climate-independent.
Core Innovation
CHP Cascade
Architecture

Combined Heat and Power cascade is the technology that makes PUE ≤ 1.05 structurally achievable at production scale. The Cat G3520H natural gas genset (2,507 kW electrical) simultaneously generates ~1,372 kW of recoverable jacket water heat (99°C) and ~1,128 kW of exhaust heat (397°C). Total system efficiency: ~88.7%. That waste heat — instead of being rejected into the atmosphere — cascades into two systems that would otherwise draw from the electrical load:

System 1 — Munters Desiccant Regeneration
Humidity Control Off the Power Bill

Exhaust heat (397°C) from the genset drives the Munters ICA desiccant regeneration cycle directly. The Munters draws nothing from the compute power budget. Dehumidification is thermodynamically free.

System 2 — LiBr Absorption Chiller
CDU Heat Rejection Driven by Waste Heat

CHP jacket water (99°C) drives a lithium bromide absorption chiller that produces chilled water for the CDU secondary (heat rejection) loop. This eliminates the compressor-based cooling that would otherwise be required to reject 2.3 MW of GPU heat to ambient. No electrical chillers, no compressor parasitic load. Both parasitic loads eliminated.

Power-source agnostic. Cat G3520H genset for initial deployment. Bloom Energy SOFC at scale. FuelCell Energy MCFC as second qualified platform. The CHP cascade works identically regardless of heat source — the thermodynamic loop closes on waste heat, not on the fuel type that created it.
