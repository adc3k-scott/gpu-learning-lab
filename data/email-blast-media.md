**Louisiana Launches First Statewide AI Infrastructure Initiative — Free Public Service to Register Every School for Federal and State Funding**

LAFAYETTE, LA — Louisiana's AI Infrastructure Initiative has launched a free public service to help every university, college, and K-12 school in the state register for AI infrastructure funding before critical federal deadlines expire.

The initiative operates at https://louisianaai.net with a 24/7 AI-assisted phone line at (337) 448-4242.

**The problem:** Florida invested $180 million and built HiPerGator — the #3 supercomputer in the world — giving all 12 Florida state universities access to GPU compute. Louisiana currently has zero dedicated GPU infrastructure serving any of its 45 universities or 69 school districts. Meanwhile, $37 billion in corporate AI compute is being built in Louisiana (Meta $27B, Hut 8 $10B, Amazon $12B) — none of it serving a single university student or K-12 classroom.

**The solution:** Two abandoned Louisiana landmarks are being repurposed as AI factory campuses:

- **Ragin' Cajun Compute Campus** — The historic Trappeys Cannery in Lafayette, closed for 30+ years, is being reborn as a solar-powered AI factory in partnership with UL Lafayette. 112,500 sq ft across 4 buildings. 2.05 MW First Solar rooftop array.

- **Tiger Compute Campus** — The former Entergy Willow Glen power station in St. Gabriel, decommissioned since 2016, is being reborn as a 700-acre AI factory campus in partnership with LSU. 500kV and 230kV electrical substations still live on-site. Mississippi River cooling. 260 MW capacity ceiling.

Both facilities use NVIDIA's latest 800V DC architecture, American-made equipment from Louisiana vendors, and self-generated power at Henry Hub natural gas pricing — the cheapest energy cost in the country.

**The urgency:** Multiple funding deadlines are expiring now:
- NVIDIA Academic Grant — March 31, 2026 (8 days)
- DOE SPARK Concept Paper — April 2, 2026 (10 days)
- Angel Investor Tax Credit — June 30, 2026
- Solar ITC Safe Harbor — July 3, 2026

19+ federal and state incentive programs are available and stack on the same projects. The initiative helps schools identify which programs they qualify for and assists with filing — all at no cost.

**This is 100% public infrastructure.** Unlike corporate AI operations that serve shareholders, this initiative serves Louisiana's schools and communities. It cannot be bought or acquired. The registration, eligibility assessment, and filing assistance are permanently free.
