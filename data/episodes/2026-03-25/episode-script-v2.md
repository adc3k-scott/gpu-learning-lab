## STORY 1 -- NVIDIA and Emerald AI: AI Factories as Grid Assets

**Anchor:** Armando at Desk
**Category:** National / Global AI Infrastructure (60%)
**Duration:** ~40 seconds

**Headline:** NVIDIA and Emerald AI Turn AI Factories Into Flexible Grid Assets
**Source:** NVIDIA Newsroom, March 23, 2026
**Article URL:** https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-and-emerald-ai-join-leading-energy-companies-to-pioneer-flexible-ai-factories-as-grid-assets
**Photo URL:** https://iprsoftwaremedia.com/219/files/202603/nvidia-emerald-ai.png
**Photo Description:** NVIDIA and Emerald AI partnership announcement graphic

> NVIDIA just redefined what an AI factory can be. At CERAWeek in Houston, NVIDIA and a startup called Emerald AI announced a partnership with six major energy companies -- AES, Constellation, Invenergy, NextEra, Nscale, and Vistra -- to build AI factories that double as flexible grid assets. The technology is called DSX Flex, built into NVIDIA's new Vera Rubin reference design. It lets AI factories ramp compute up or down in real time based on grid demand. Emerald AI claims this approach could unlock up to one hundred gigawatts of capacity across the US power system. That is not a typo. One hundred gigawatts. The first commercial deployment is planned for NVIDIA's Virginia AI Factory Research Center later this year. This is the energy-first model we have been talking about -- and now NVIDIA is building the software to make it standard.

## STORY 2 -- Musk Launches $25B Terafab in Austin

**Anchor:** Armando at Desk
**Category:** National / Global AI Infrastructure (60%)
**Duration:** ~40 seconds

**Headline:** Elon Musk Launches $25 Billion Terafab Chip Factory in Austin
**Source:** Electrek / Bloomberg, March 22, 2026
**Article URL:** https://electrek.co/2026/03/22/tesla-spacex-terafab-chip-factory-ai-desperation/
**Photo URL:** https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/03/Tesla-SpaceX-terafab-e1774190764202.jpeg?quality=82&strip=all&w=1600
**Photo Description:** Terafab launch event at the former Seaholm Power Plant in Austin, Texas

> Elon Musk stood on stage at a decommissioned power plant in Austin last Friday and announced what he called the most epic chip building exercise in history. Terafab is a twenty-five billion dollar semiconductor fab -- a joint venture between Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI -- targeting two-nanometer process technology under one roof. Design, lithography, fabrication, memory, packaging, and testing. All of it. The first chip off the line will be AI5, Tesla's fifth-generation inference chip, with small batches targeted for late this year. Here is the number that matters: Musk says eighty percent of Terafab's output goes to space -- orbital AI satellites. Twenty percent stays on the ground. He is not competing with NVIDIA for your AI factory. He is building his own supply chain for orbit. Whether he can actually execute a bleeding-edge fab from scratch -- that is the trillion-dollar question.

## STORY 3 -- Eaton Beam Rubin DSX: 800-Volt DC Power Standard

**Anchor:** Adriana in the Field (Houston / GTC set)
**Category:** National / Global AI Infrastructure (60%)
**Duration:** ~35 seconds

**Headline:** Eaton and NVIDIA Unveil 800-Volt DC Power Platform for AI Factories
**Source:** Data Center Frontier / Eaton Newsroom, March 2026
**Article URL:** https://www.datacenterfrontier.com/energy/article/55323139/preparing-for-800-vdc-data-centers-abb-eaton-support-nvidias-ai-infrastructure-evolution
**Photo URL:** https://img.datacenterfrontier.com/files/base/ebm/datacenterfrontier/image/2025/10/68efe50393f029132ab6b9c6-20251014_131034.png?auto=format,compress&fit=fill&fill=blur&q=45?w=250&width=250
**Photo Description:** Eaton 800 VDC sidecar prototype hardware on display

> Adriana here. If NVIDIA is building the brain of the AI factory, Eaton just built the spine. The Beam Rubin DSX is a modular, pre-engineered power platform designed specifically for NVIDIA's NVL72 racks running on eight hundred volts DC. That is a major shift. Traditional facilities convert power from AC to DC multiple times before it reaches the GPU. Each conversion wastes energy as heat. Eight hundred volt DC eliminates those stages. Eaton says it can support rack densities above one hundred thirty kilowatts -- nearly triple what facilities handled just a few years ago. The ecosystem already includes ABB, Delta, Schneider Electric, Texas Instruments, Infineon, and Vertiv. Over two hundred partners. This is not a prototype. This is the new power standard for every AI factory being built from here forward. Back to you, Armando.

## STORY 5 -- FAA Part 108: BVLOS Drone Rule Approaching Final

**Anchor:** Armando at Desk
**Category:** Emerging Tech (10%)
**Duration:** ~30 seconds

**Headline:** FAA Part 108 BVLOS Drone Rule Nears Final Publication
**Source:** Aviation International News / FAA, Spring 2026
**Article URL:** https://www.ainonline.com/aviation-news/futureflight/2026-01-01/faas-bvlos-overhaul-approaches-finish-line
**Photo URL:** https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/fact_sheets/Fact_Sheet_BVLOS.pdf
**Photo Description:** FAA BVLOS fact sheet (use FAA logo or drone in flight image)

> And finally, the rule that the entire drone industry has been waiting for. The FAA's Part 108 -- the framework that legalizes beyond-visual-line-of-sight drone operations at scale -- is expected to reach final publication this spring. The comment period closed last October with over three thousand responses. The framework creates two tiers: operating permits for lower-risk flights and operating certificates for complex missions. It covers drones up to thirteen hundred twenty pounds and replaces the current waiver-by-waiver system that has bottlenecked commercial drone ops for years. When this rule drops, companies like Skydio, FlytBase, and every first responder drone program in the country will have a clear legal path to fly autonomously. This is the unlock.
