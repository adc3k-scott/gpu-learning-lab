## 1. NSF MRI (Major Research Instrumentation Program)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | Major Research Instrumentation Program (MRI) |
| **Administering Agency** | National Science Foundation (NSF), Office of Integrative Activities (OIA) |
| **Where to File** | Research.gov (https://www.research.gov) or Grants.gov (https://www.grants.gov) |
| **Application Format** | Full proposal via Research.gov or Grants.gov electronic submission |
| **Deadlines** | **FY 2026 window CANCELLED** — no new proposals accepted during Oct 15 - Nov 14, 2025 window. Next window: **October 15 - November 16, 2026** (for FY 2027 awards). Proposals submitted outside the window are returned without review. |
| **Eligibility** | U.S. institutions of higher education (IHEs), non-profit non-academic institutions, and consortia thereof. Each performing organization may submit no more than **2 proposals in Track 1** and **1 in Track 2**. Internal limited-submission competition required at most universities. |
| **Funding Levels** | Track 1: $100K - $1.4M. Track 2: $1.4M - $4M. Track 3: $100K - $4M (helium conservation). |
| **Required Documents** | Project description, budget (with 30% cost-share from non-federal sources for doctoral institutions), letters of support from senior administration, instrument specifications, vendor quotes, institutional 10-digit ID number, PI biographical sketches, current & pending support, data management plan, facilities/equipment description. |
| **Processing Time** | ~6-9 months from submission to award notification. Proposals reviewed by panels; awards typically announced the following spring/summer. |
| **Contact** | NSF OIA/IA, 703-292-5111, mriquestions@nsf.gov |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **30% cost-share required** for doctoral-granting institutions — this is the #1 reason proposals fail. Non-doctoral institutions are exempt from cost-share. (2) Internal limited-submission competition — the university's research office must select which proposals go forward. Start internal process 3-4 months before NSF deadline. (3) GPU clusters absolutely qualify — prior awards include GPU accelerated computing cores (Award #1827314). (4) FY 2026 is DARK — no proposals accepted. Plan for the Oct 2026 window now. |

**URL:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/mri-major-research-instrumentation-program

## 4. Federal Solar ITC (30% Investment Tax Credit)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | Investment Tax Credit (ITC) under IRC Section 48 / Section 48E (Clean Electricity Investment Credit) |
| **Administering Agency** | IRS (Internal Revenue Service) |
| **Where to File** | Claimed on federal tax return using **IRS Form 3468** (Investment Credit) + **Form 3800** (General Business Credit) |
| **Application Format** | Tax return filing — no separate application required. For direct pay election (nonprofits/tax-exempt entities): pre-filing registration via IRA/CHIPS Pre-Filing Registration Tool at https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/register-for-elective-payment |
| **Deadlines** | **CRITICAL: Construction must begin by July 3, 2026** to lock in 30% credit. Alternative: project placed in service by end of 2027. |
| **Eligibility** | Any taxpayer placing qualifying solar property in service. For-profit and nonprofit (via direct pay). |
| **Safe Harbor for "Begin Construction"** | For projects **1.5 MW and smaller**: spend 5% of total project costs on equipment by July 3, 2026 (physical work or 5% safe harbor). For projects **larger than 1.5 MW**: must begin actual physical work — 5% safe harbor no longer available. |
| **Required Documents** | Documentation proving construction start date: contracts, purchase orders, delivery receipts, site photos with dates, construction logs, invoices showing 5% spend. Keep ALL records — IRS audits construction-start claims. |
| **Processing Time** | Credit claimed on annual tax return. Refund timeline depends on filing. |
| **Contact** | IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: 800-829-4933 |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **FEOC restrictions starting 2026** — components from China, Iran, North Korea, Russia may disqualify. Projects starting construction after 12/31/2025 must comply. First Solar (US-made) panels are FEOC-compliant. (2) Bonus adders: +10% for domestic content, +10% for energy communities, +10-20% for low-income communities = up to 70% total credit. (3) Must file for domestic content bonus separately. (4) If you miss the July 3, 2026 deadline, the credit phases down or converts to the new 48E structure with different rules. (5) Tax-exempt entities (universities) use direct pay — must register BEFORE filing. |

**Bonus Adders Available:**
- Domestic Content: +10% (use First Solar panels = automatic qualification)
- Energy Community: +10% (brownfield sites, fossil fuel communities)
- Low-Income: +10-20% (allocated by DOE, competitive application)

**URL:** https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/investment-tax-credit

## 5. 100% Bonus Depreciation (OBBBA)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | First-Year Bonus Depreciation under IRC Section 168(k), restored by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA, P.L. 119-21) |
| **Administering Agency** | IRS |
| **Where to File** | Claimed on federal tax return using **IRS Form 4562** (Depreciation and Amortization) |
| **Application Format** | Tax return election — no separate application. Bonus depreciation is AUTOMATIC unless you elect out. |
| **Deadlines** | Applies to qualifying assets acquired and placed in service **on or after January 19, 2025**. Permanent — no sunset. |
| **Eligibility** | Any business taxpayer. Property must be: new or used (first use by the taxpayer), with a recovery period of 20 years or less. Includes computers, servers, GPUs, networking equipment, HVAC, electrical infrastructure, qualified improvement property, software. |
| **Required Documents** | Purchase contracts showing acquisition date on/after Jan 19, 2025. Placed-in-service documentation. Asset lists with cost basis. Both acquisition date AND placed-in-service date must be on/after January 19, 2025. |
| **Processing Time** | Applied on annual tax return. |
| **Contact** | IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: 800-829-4933; consult CPA |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **Both acquisition AND placed-in-service dates must be on/after January 19, 2025** — a contract signed before that date may not qualify even if equipment arrives later. (2) This is 100% first-year expensing — entire cost deducted in year 1. (3) Can elect to take less than 100% if it creates an NOL you can't use. (4) Election out is per-class, per-year — irrevocable. (5) IRS Notice 2026-11 provides interim guidance — read it. (6) GPU servers, networking switches, cooling equipment, solar panels (if not using ITC), electrical gear all qualify. (7) Can stack with ITC — but basis must be reduced by 50% of ITC before applying depreciation. |

**IRS Guidance:** Notice 2026-11 (interim guidance on OBBBA bonus depreciation rules)

## 6. Opportunity Zones (Qualified Opportunity Fund)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | Qualified Opportunity Zone / Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF) under IRC Sections 1400Z-1 and 1400Z-2 |
| **Administering Agency** | IRS |
| **Where to File** | Self-certification via **IRS Form 8996** filed with the entity's federal tax return. Investors file **Form 8949** (capital gains deferral election) + **Form 8997** (annual QOF investment statement). |
| **Application Format** | Self-certification — NO pre-approval needed. Entity files Form 8996 with its tax return. |
| **Setup Process** | (1) Form a corporation or partnership (LLC taxed as either). (2) Organize under state law for purpose of investing in QOZ property. (3) File Form 8996 with the entity's first federal tax return, specifying the month QOF status begins. (4) Maintain 90% asset test. |
| **Deadlines** | Rolling — investors must invest capital gains within **180 days** of realizing the gain. QOF must file Form 8996 annually. 90% asset test measured at mid-year and year-end. |
| **Eligibility** | Any corporation or partnership organized to invest in Qualified Opportunity Zone property. Must hold at least 90% of assets in QOZ property. Trappeys site should be checked against the QOZ map. |
| **Required Documents** | Form 8996 (annually), organizational documents showing QOZ investment purpose, asset valuations at test dates, property records proving QOZ location. |
| **Processing Time** | Self-certification — effective immediately upon filing. |
| **Contact** | IRS OZ hotline, https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/opportunity-zones |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **No pre-approval needed** — just self-certify on the tax return. (2) Monthly penalty if you fail the 90% asset test. (3) The entity must be organized for QOZ investment — can't retrofit an existing operating company. (4) Must invest in QOZ *property* — the land/building must be in a designated zone. Verify zone status at https://opportunityzones.hud.gov. (5) Original capital gains deferral benefits expired 12/31/2026 under TCJA, but check for OBBBA extensions. (6) QOZ designation itself may have been extended — verify current status. |

**Key Forms:**
- Form 8996: QOF self-certification + annual 90% test
- Form 8949: Investor capital gains deferral election
- Form 8997: Investor annual QOF investment statement

**URL:** https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/businesses/certify-and-maintain-a-qualified-opportunity-fund

## 7. New Markets Tax Credits (NMTC)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | New Markets Tax Credit Program |
| **Administering Agency** | U.S. Treasury, Community Development Financial Institutions Fund (CDFI Fund) |
| **Where to File** | AMIS portal (Awards Management Information System) at https://amis.cdfifund.gov |
| **Application Format** | Two-step: (1) CDE Certification application, then (2) NMTC Allocation application — both via AMIS |
| **Deadlines** | **CDE Certification for 2026 round: must submit by November 7, 2025** (this deadline has PASSED). CDE Certification applications are rolling — apply ASAP for future rounds. 2026 allocation round: applications expected to open mid-2026, with up to $5B in allocations. |
| **Eligibility** | Applicants must be certified as a **Community Development Entity (CDE)**. CDEs must: (1) have a primary mission of serving low-income communities, (2) maintain accountability to residents of low-income communities. Projects must be in qualifying census tracts. |
| **How It Actually Works** | You don't apply directly. You partner with an existing CDE that has NMTC allocation. The CDE invests in your project via a Qualified Low-Income Community Investment (QLICI). The CDE's investors receive a 39% tax credit over 7 years. |
| **Required Documents** | For CDE certification: articles of incorporation/organization, board composition, community advisory board documentation. For allocation: detailed application through AMIS showing track record, investment strategy, community impact. |
| **Processing Time** | CDE Certification: rolling, typically 30-60 days. Allocation rounds: 6-12 months. |
| **Contact** | CDFI Fund Help Desk: 202-653-0421 (option 3), cdfihelp@cdfi.treas.gov |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **Most organizations should NOT become a CDE** — instead, partner with an existing CDE that already has allocation. (2) Find CDEs at https://www.cdfifund.gov/programs-training/certification/cde. (3) NMTC deals are complex — require tax counsel and 7-year compliance period. (4) Can be combined with Historic Tax Credits and ITC for massive stacking. (5) All FY 2025-2026 R&D credits are GONE — plan for FY 2026-2027 cycle. |

**URL:** https://www.cdfifund.gov/programs-training/programs/new-markets-tax-credit

## 8. CHIPS Act (Section 48D Advanced Manufacturing Investment Credit)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Exact Name** | Advanced Manufacturing Investment Credit (IRC Section 48D), established by CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 |
| **Administering Agency** | IRS + NIST CHIPS for America (for direct funding) |
| **Where to File** | Tax credit: IRS Form 3468 + Form 3800. Pre-filing registration required via IRA/CHIPS Pre-Filing Registration Tool. Direct funding applications: https://www.nist.gov/chips |
| **Application Format** | Tax credit claimed on return. Direct funding: application via CHIPS for America portal. |
| **Deadlines** | **CRITICAL: Construction must begin BEFORE December 31, 2026** for the tax credit. Credit rate increased to **35%** (up from 25%) by OBBBA for property placed in service after 12/31/2025. |
| **Eligibility** | Facilities whose **primary purpose is manufacturing of semiconductors or semiconductor manufacturing equipment**. Must be tangible depreciable property. AI infrastructure (data centers, GPU clusters) does NOT directly qualify UNLESS tied to semiconductor manufacturing. |
| **Required Documents** | IRA/CHIPS Pre-Filing Registration (get registration number), Form 3468, Form 3800, documentation of qualifying investment, facility purpose documentation. |
| **Processing Time** | Tax credit on annual return. Direct funding awards: 6-18 months. |
| **Contact** | CHIPS for America: chips@nist.gov, https://www.nist.gov/chips |
| **Gotchas** | (1) **AI factories/data centers do NOT directly qualify** for the 48D credit — it's for semiconductor manufacturing. (2) OpenAI and others are lobbying to expand 48D to AI data centers — watch for legislative changes. (3) The direct CHIPS funding ($39B in subsidies) is for semiconductor fabs. (4) HOWEVER: if a facility manufactures AI accelerators or semiconductor manufacturing equipment, it qualifies. (5) The 35% rate (OBBBA increase) applies to property placed in service after 12/31/2025. (6) Trump administration is actively promoting data center siting on brownfield/Superfund sites via executive order — separate from CHIPS but related. |

**More relevant for AI infrastructure — CHIPS R&D Programs:**
- NIST CHIPS R&D: https://www.nist.gov/chips
- National Semiconductor Technology Center (NSTC)
- CHIPS Manufacturing USA institutes
- These may fund AI-adjacent semiconductor research at universities

**URL:** https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/advanced-manufacturing-investment-credit
