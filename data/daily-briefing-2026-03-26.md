### 2. PJM Grid Fails Reliability Target: 6.6 GW Shortfall Confirmed for 2027
**Source**: [Spotlight PA](https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2026/01/pjm-grid-short-fall-power-plants-data-centers-environment/) | [Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/pjm-interconnection-capacity-auction-data-center/808264/) | [Introl](https://introl.com/blog/pjm-grid-crisis-2027-data-center-capacity-shortfall) | Cited at CERAWeek Day 3-4

For the first time in its history, PJM Interconnection -- the nation's largest grid operator serving 65 million people across 13 states -- failed to procure enough capacity to meet reliability targets. The December 2025 auction came up 6,625 MW short for the 2027/28 delivery year. Data centers account for 94% of projected load growth in PJM territory. Capacity prices spiked to a record $333/MW-day, up from $28.92 just two years prior. PJM forecasts adding 5-7 GW of data center load but only 2-3 GW of new supply annually through 2032. Gartner now predicts power shortages will constrain 40% of AI facilities by 2027.

**ADC Relevance**: **CRITICAL** -- This is the single strongest validation of ADC's behind-the-meter, energy-first model. Grid-dependent AI factories face rolling blackout risk and skyrocketing capacity costs. ADC's 4-layer power hierarchy (solar + gas + diesel + grid sell-back) avoids this entirely. Willow Glen and Trappeys are not grid-dependent. This story should be in every investor deck, every city council presentation, every NVIDIA conversation. "We don't need PJM. We make our own power."

### 5. Vineland NJ: Community Revolt Against 350 MW AI Facility -- A Cautionary Tale
**Source**: [WHYY/NPR](https://whyy.org/articles/data-center-artificial-intelligence-vineland-new-jersey/) | [NJ1015](https://nj1015.com/vineland-nj-data-center-concerns/) | [Press of Atlantic City](https://pressofatlanticcity.com/news/local/government-politics/article_14f92b0c-9e8a-4861-a081-c4f8ae4e77a8.html) | March 25

A Nebius Group / Microsoft-backed 350 MW AI facility in Vineland, New Jersey (part of a $17B deal) is facing organized community backlash. Over 100 residents protested on March 25, citing a constant low-frequency hum from construction, threats to the Kirkwood-Cohansey aquifer (South Jersey's primary drinking water source), and lack of notification before approval. The project sits in an Urban Enterprise Zone, which bypassed normal resident notification requirements. Gas-fired power, noise, and water usage are the flashpoints. The facility is on track for end-of-2026 operations despite opposition.

**ADC Relevance**: **HIGH** -- This is how NOT to build an AI factory. ADC's approach is the opposite: community-first (Trappeys preserves a historic cannery), renewable-first (solar primary, gas backbone -- not gas-only), transparent (city council presentations, university partnership, first responder integration), and water-smart (closed-loop liquid cooling, water tower heritage feature). Use Vineland as a contrast story: "They dropped a gas plant on a community. We're building a solar AI factory WITH our community." Every investor and city council member needs to see this comparison.
